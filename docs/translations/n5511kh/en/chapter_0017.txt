# Chapter 14

<b>

<b>

"Lady Witch, there's a bedroom in the back! I'll get it ready right away so you can use it!"

<b>

( No, I don't sleep in a bed, so it's fine )

<b>

<b>

Noel, who had apparently thoroughly searched the house, was enthusiastic, so I shook my head. Lying down on a bed would put my face low. In other words, it would put my face at a height where Noel might accidentally touch it.

<b>

( It would be terrible if he accidentally took a suction, after all. If I can't be buried in soil, I'll rest somewhere else )

<b>

Passing by Noel, who was tilting his head in confusion, I entered the room. The nearer room looked like a spacious living room. I touched the wall and grew plants that put down roots into it; in no time vines covered the walls and ceiling. That alone felt a bit plain, so I made sure some flowers bloomed too. …Yeah, it's starting to give off the vibe of a witch's house. I'll grow more plants here and there later.

<b>

( I think I'll make my bed in that ceiling. …Ah, right. A hammock )

<b>

So I grew the vines up to the ceiling and wove them into a complicated hanging hammock. At this height there was no way someone could accidentally brush against me. I didn't make a ladder to climb up; I planned to use my own vines each time. This should be a perfect countermeasure.

<b>

<b>

"Wow… sleeping in that looks so comfortable, Lady Witch."

<b>

(Hm? If Noel wants a hammock too, should I make one for him?)

<b>

<b>

I went into the adjacent bedroom. A simple bed sat by the window and nothing else; since there was nothing else, it might be best to let Noel use it however he liked.

I made a hammock for Noel next to the bed just in case. He looked at me with sparkling eyes, so I nodded to indicate he could use it. …Maybe that bed will never be used.

<b>

<b>

"Ah—Lady Witch, you're really amazing. This house already has a very different atmosphere… you can make anything from plants, and if you can prepare what you need yourself like this, you wouldn't have had to come down to the human village at all."

<b>

( Yeah, let's make that the setting… Actually I came down because I was in trouble, though )

<b>

<b>

Days of fighting monsters, absorbing them. What a grim life that had been. Humans didn't attack me—some mistook me for a kindly witch, so they weren't frightened of me.

This could be called the safe place I'd wanted. I'd like to keep living here pretending to be human.

<b>

<b>

"But why, after being away from the human world for five hundred years, would you come to a place like this…"

<b>

<b>

Gulp. Indeed, if a witch had been hiding for five hundred years, it would be strange for her to suddenly appear. If asked why I came suddenly, the truth was I had simply fled from monsters. Well, I couldn't speak anyway, so there was no way to explain.

<b>

(That mountain's full of zombie-type monsters that don't taste good at all… that's also a pretty major reason )

<b>

At some point the monsters that appeared had become half-rotten things, zombie-like monsters. They were completely unpalatable even when absorbed. Their nutrients were far less than other monsters; it felt like I was eating nothing but the husks.

<b>

I found myself gazing distantly toward the mountain I had come down from. Noel and Daon followed my gaze.

<b>

<b>

"…Come to think of it, Lady Witch, you came down from the mountain?"

<b>

"From that mountain? No way… You lived in a demon realm?"

<b>

( Ah, damn, I blurted it out…W-what if they find out I'm a monster…?! )

<b>

<b>

Aaaah, I screamed inside my body, but to them my face probably looked like I was smiling. And that smile seemed to be taken as confirmation, because Daon looked at me as if seeing something outrageous—basically, a look of utter horror.

<b>

<b>

"Even a witch with great magical power—what recklessness… Have you been watching over the land where your kin sleep for five hundred years?"

<b>

( Huh? …It seems the conversation's heading in a weird direction…? )

<b>

"They say only about five hundred of the demon tribe remained after the battle with the dragon. …Could it be you were mourning all your kin?"

<b>

<b>

Their words taught me that in the monster realm where I was born, the demon tribe had fought the dragon and been wiped out. That land had been so full of magic that maybe that's why I was so round and plump like a turnip. …The ground around there tasted so good.

Since they were misunderstanding in my favor, I nodded. Better that than being thought a monster.

<b>

<b>

"You're kind, Lady Witch… so you prayed for your kin all this time. But I heard creatures can't live in places thick with miasma. That's why our village couldn't be built that high up."

<b>

"Exactly. Even demonkind could be harmed—no wonder you couldn't stay in the center. The effects of such intense magic on the body are immeasurable… You couldn't have been at the very heart, but still, to continue living near that land is admirable. I am in awe. However, when the miasma wanes, many monsters should appear—wasn't that dangerous?"

<b>

"Oh, and before you came here, Lady Witch, you defeated some incredible beast. You're not only kind but very powerful."

<b>

"I see. Having survived the battle with the dragon, you must indeed have been a special witch. A witch who could live despite the drifting miasma…"

<b>

<b>

Because I couldn't say anything, Noel and Daon took it upon themselves to invent and expand explanations. They were trying to make plausible reasons for why a witch like me could continue living in the monster realm.

<b>

( I see, if we go with that it's not unnatural… good, go ahead, make up more backstory )

<b>

Well, when I think about it, it probably is easier to come up with a reason why a witch lived for so long in some hidden place than to accept the absurd truth that the person standing in front of them is actually a monster mandragora merely taking human form.

Unless a monster had memories from when it used to be human, I can’t imagine it would ever go out of its way to spend evolution points to become a person. I’m just in an unusual situation.

<b>

And so, after the two of them talked it over, my backstory became this: I was an ancient Flower Witch, a compassionate witch who had spent five hundred years mourning all five hundred of her kin who died fighting the dragon. Recently, the demon realm had grown so overrun with monsters that I finally judged it too dangerous, decided to return to the human world, and came down from the mountain. ...If a witch like that actually existed, she’d be something else.

<b>

<b>

"Lady Witch, although it's hidden now by weeds, there used to be a field next door. You seem to like flowers, so perhaps planting something would suit you… Please make the house and surroundings comfortable to live in."

<b>

( Oh, right. I should think about Noel's meals too… I'll grow a lot of edible plants )

<b>

"Please, take your time to rest. Before long we'll hold a feast to show our gratitude to you, so we hope you'll come when that happens."

<b>

<b>

With that, Daon returned to the village. Noel and I remained; first we cleared the weeds around the house and grew various edible plants to make our dinner—but then—

<b>

<b>

"…I'm sorry… I should have been cured by the witch's medicine, so why…"

<b>

( Just rest for a while. You're tired today )

<b>

Noel, who had washed the harvested fruits and vegetables clean in the river and eaten them fresh, threw up like the villagers had. A complete recovery potion brought the color back to his face and restored his energy, but to be safe we decided to let him rest in the room.

<b>

( …I wonder what's causing this illness )

<b>

Maybe people in the village were getting sick again. Although complete recovery potions are easy to make, they still consume my magic and make me hungry. Since Noel wasn't being watched and poisonous fish would be fine for me, I went into the river to catch some when—

<b>

( Ugh, this water tastes awful! It's got that zombie-monster bitterness! )

<b>

Water soaking through the gaps in my clothes tasted foul and made me grimace. Then I realized—…could this be the cause of the sickness?

<b>
