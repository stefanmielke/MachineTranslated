# Chapter 15

<b>

<b>

(If the river water tastes like that zombie-y flavor, then it's probably because that rotten-smelling monster ichor is flowing downstream. It's no wonder humans' stomachs get upset, even if I can eat it without trouble.)

<b>

<b>

I stretched my vine-clothes where no one was watching, snagged a fish in the water, pulled it back, and let it brush my feet under my skirt as I ate and thought. Noel said poisonous fish had increased, and it seems that was true. I get that poisonous fish don't taste great, but more than half of them feel like they're poisonous now. The water must be bad, affecting the fish. And these aren't ordinary fish—they're a kind of monster that evolved to withstand this poisonous water. …Well, even if they're poisonous, they're still monsters, so they're more energizing than regular fish, and fine for my meals.

<b>

<b>

(Hmm, but this isn't good. Noel getting sick was probably from washing food in this river… are the villagers the same? So if we don't deal with the root cause, this illness won't go away.)

<b>

<b>

I turned my gaze upstream. There were a lot more zombie monsters higher up the mountain, and wiping them all out would be hard. Voice-based attacks don't work on them, so we'd have to take them down one by one, and that's just impossible.

<b>

<b>

(But if things stay like this, no ordinary animals — humans included — will be able to live around this mountain. Daon said that the ecosystem there is still forming, so nobody really knows what changes will happen…)

<b>

<b>

From bits of conversation I overheard while the two of them were building up the witch backstory, I gathered that the mountain had been filled with too much mana because a dragon died there, and that excess became miasma that harmed living things. Over five hundred years that faded, and now monsters are hyperactive there, a dangerous place where monsters eat each other and evolve into new forms. Humans call such places a demon realm.

<b>

<b>

(Oh good, I'm glad I escaped before it became swarming with powerful monsters… That mountain turned into a zombie-filled ecosystem because of this so-called new evolution. …At first it was just goblins and the like, but eventually there were only zombie goblins.)

<b>

<b>

I nostalgically remembered life back then. I hated goblins because they pulled me up out of the ground to scare me. After I gained a humanoid form they stopped doing that, but when I had only my head sticking out of the ground a zombie goblin bit at my stem. Of course I screamed, but screams don't affect zombies. And zombies aren't tasty, so I didn't feel like eating them— I tightened them with my hair-vines and spat digestive fluid, but they swarmed, and tearing was quicker than dissolving, so I pulled them apart with vines, ripped them with sharp-leaved plants, and fought like that—

<b>

<b>

(Ugh, what a terrible memory. I never want to go back to that life… Anyway, since the mountain's swarming with zombies, it's no surprise the water's polluted. I have to purify it.)

<b>

<b>

There are, of course, plants that can purify polluted water. I sat on the riverbank and grew plants I had created through diversification around me, ordering them to reach upstream. Growth has its limits, and I could make more by using mana, but using too much would make me hungry.

Because this spot is upstream of the village, I extended them toward both the village and the mountain and stopped when they'd covered the village area. …I feel like most of what I replenished with fish is gone.

<b>

<b>

(I can't do it all at once… I'll just stretch it out over time. There were no zombies partway down the mountain, so I can aim for around there… Oh, right. I should probably reach about as far as where that person was lying down.)

<b>

<b>

The first human I helped after taking human form. I wonder how that knight is doing now. I steered clear so he wouldn't wake up and see me, and I don't know if he passed me while I was moving on. Did he make it home alive, or did he die? I hope he's alive, but he didn't seem to be from this village, so we probably won't meet again and there's no way to check.

<b>

<b>

(But humans go that far upstream, right? So I should purify the water up to that point. It'll help animals that aren't tolerant to the poison…)

<b>

<b>

I also need to check how the village uses water. And tomorrow I need to hand out more healing potions to those who are still unwell.

<b>

<b>

(There was a well in the village. Groundwater can take years to resurface, so well water might be okay… but I'll grow these plants there just in case.)

<b>

<b>

Plants with many small white flowers were growing by the water. Where I found them was on the backs of zombie monsters.

They parasitized zombies like some kind of Cordyceps and multiplied. When the whole body became covered in flowers, the zombie's body crumbled back to soil and the flowers put down roots— that was the plant. Because I can display plant information I assimilate as a status, I can know detailed things about anything I take in.

I thought this would be an effective attack against zombies and assimilated it, but it only parasitizes zombies, so it's safe for humans. …The zombies' fate is pretty gruesome, but it doesn't endanger people.

<b>

<b>

(Really, this world has scary plants… but they're useful for humans.)

<b>

<b>

They take in rotten moisture, waste, and toxins and turn them into nutrient-rich fertilizer. Its name is the Purifying Flower, a blunt name but a very useful plant. It seems to only live in places with especially abundant mana, so you rarely find it in human-inhabited areas, and if you bring one home and plant it, it won't spread because of the mana shortage.

My plan is to plant these in the village well, then use them in the outhouse or cesspit to make fertilizer. If I grow the plants to a certain size and then let villagers rely on the fertilizer, that should be efficient.

<b>

<b>

(If it's me, I can grow plants freely and use as many as I want, so this will really help the villagers!)

<b>

<b>

Having thought out the cause of the sickness and how to fix it, I happily returned home. …If I'm this useful a witch, surely the villagers will accept me going forward.

<b>

I used vines to climb up to the hammock hanging from the ceiling and sprawled out. …Being suspended feels strangely nice. I'm naturally a creature of the soil, not meant to be hung in the air. For a moment I pictured the image of a root vegetable being hung up in my old world.

<b>

(breathless, musing) (I wonder if dried daikon feels like this…)

<b>
