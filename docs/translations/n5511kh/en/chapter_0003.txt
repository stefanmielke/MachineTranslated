# Chapter 3

<b>

<b>

<b>

A monster's body grows into the shape it desires. Realizing that made me able to hold onto hope.

If humans or similar intelligent beings exist in this world, wouldn't living among them let me have a peaceful life? If I can grow toward the shape I wish for, someday I should be able to take that form.

<b>

(Okay, aim for human! …Although, the only human-like thing I've seen is that huge green giant… There's no way that's a human in this world, right?)

<b>

The green-skinned giant lying by the water was dead, foam at its mouth and eyes rolled back. It had been the thing that pulled me out and scared me so much.

When I got a proper look at it for the first time, its skin was green with a pitch-black patch on its chest, small horns on its brow, and it wore only a ragged cloth around its waist. It didn't look very intelligent.

<b>

(It looks more like an oni than a human. A green oni… a goblin? But goblins are supposed to be small…)

<b>

I thought maybe goblins in this world were large, but after all, I'm not human — I'm a mandragora, a mandrake. It wouldn't be strange if goblins were bigger.

If that's the case, humans would probably be even larger. …I need to properly confirm what humans look like before I can change. First of all, I have to find out whether humans even exist here.

<b>

<b>

"…? …!"

<b>

<b>

I heard voices coming from somewhere, so I ran into the bushes and hid. Was it the green giant, or something else? I peeked out silently from the bushes to see.

<b>

<b>

"Honestly, that court mage is awful at delegating…! Sending us into a monster-infested wasteland like this…!"

<b>

<b>

Two people walked by speaking a language I could understand. Good — there was no language barrier. And they looked like humans I knew, no different, and I almost shouted "Yay!" from joy, so I hurriedly clapped my webbed hand over my mouth. …I managed to hold back before any sound came out. I'm really glad I have webbed hands.

<b>

<b>

"We couldn't have come this far on our own. But the mage's research is for the kingdom, for the people. It's only natural a holy knight would lend a hand."

<b>

"You're at it again… truly, you are the ideal knight, aren't you?"

<b>

<b>

The two, who appeared to be adult men, were even taller than the green giant. Even if that giant stood up, it wouldn't reach their waists. I estimated their size by comparing them to the surrounding trees.

Now that my target form was clearer, I felt unexpectedly happy. Having a concrete goal really motivates me.

<b>

(Since I'm female, make me shorter than those guys… Wait, I'm a girl, right?)

<b>

I don't know if mandrakes have sexes. Whether they have separate male and female plants, or both stamens and pistils in one. Either way, with my previous life's consciousness as Hanazono Misaki, I recognize myself as female. So I'll make a female body.

<b>

(If I'm going to make a body… I want it to be, like, model-level, right…?)

<b>

In my past life I was boxy, and now I'm a round body that rolls forever, but since I can change into whatever I want now, if I'm going to aim for something it'd be a stunning beauty. Big chest, a narrow waist, a rounded rear. That's the body I want.

<b>

(Luckily I confirmed humans, so I should leave before they find me. If I shout I might kill them, so I must go.)

<b>

I ran full speed from the spot. Defeat monsters, level up, someday become human, and live in a human kingdom. Since they called this a monster-infested wasteland, the human kingdom must be a safe place.

Right after I started running, I thought I heard a surprised shout from behind, but it must have been my imagination, so I kept running. I had to get far enough away that those two couldn't be affected if I defeated a monster nearby. Whatever, just get away.

<b>

(All right, let's do this!)

<b>

<b>

―――――――

<b>

<b>

"Ah, hey…! Just now, some really… really nice-bodied mandrake ran by…!"

<b>

<b>

Leohardt, who had come to the wasteland to recover experimental specimens on behalf of court mage Nicolaus, began worrying about his colleague when that colleague pointed into empty space and said something strange.

<b>

<b>

"…Ritter, are you all right? They said the miasma had thinned, but not to the point of hallucinations…"

<b>

<b>

Until just a few years ago, this place's miasma — an overly dense magic that harmed living things — made it nearly impossible for most creatures to approach. The miasma came from the corpse of a dragon that died five hundred years ago, and its effects meant humans, animals, and even monsters couldn't live here. Only recently has it started to fade.

Now it's become fertile land brimming with magic. New plants sprout, and monsters, not animals, multiply. Lots of weak monsters are born and eat each other as they grow, eventually giving rise to stronger species. This place is currently a monster frontier building a new ecosystem.

<b>

<b>

"No, it definitely ran by!"

<b>

"Even in a wasteland, mandrakes don't run."

<b>

"Bu-but… the mage said intelligent monsters can choose an evolutionary path… a running mandrake wouldn't be impossible…!"

<b>

"That's probably why you're hallucinating. Let's finish the job and go home quickly."

<b>

<b>

Even if a running mandrake could possibly appear, this wasteland is still too new for such an anomalous creature to have arisen. Mutations like that usually take decades or centuries.

Still, on their way to the appointed spot the two stumbled upon a strange sight.

<b>

<b>

"This is odd… Goblins are dying en masse."

<b>

"Yes. And this… this is a curse. Goblins are vulnerable to curses, but this many… it's abnormal."

<b>

<b>

Seeing the black stains spreading across the goblins' chests made it clear they had died from a curse. Goblin corpses lay all over the area. They had already found more than thirty similar bodies, and the sight made Leohardt frown.

For every goblin you see, there are fifty. They breed that prolifically. If they were just walking straight toward their destination and saw this many bodies, it could be hundreds or more.

<b>

<b>

"I'm immune to curses myself, but… Ritter, you should be careful. This could be fatal. Prepare holy water and make it so necromantic monsters can't get near."

<b>

"…Hey, do you really think there was a running mandrake? Like, if one ran while screaming, goblins with no curse resistance would end up like this…"

<b>

"It's unrealistic. It's more reasonable to think a necromantic monster skilled in curses has appeared."

<b>

<b>

They continued with caution until they reached the place Nicolaus had specified: where the dragon's carcass had been. Five hundred years on, the body was gone, but bones peeked through grass swaying in the wind.

The job Nicolaus had requested was to collect the mandrakes that had grown nourished by the dragon's carcass.

<b>

<b>

"If he planted them himself, he could've come collect them."

<b>

"The mage must be busy. Maybe he can't leave his work."

<b>

"You should learn to be more suspicious of people, really…"

<b>

<b>

They split up to search for the mandrakes Nicolaus had planted. But they found none. What they did find were goblin corpses killed by a curse, and an empty hole where something had been.

<b>

<b>

"…Leohardt. This…"

<b>

"Ritter, I know what you're thinking. …First, we report to the mage."

<b>

<b>

Someone might have taken the mandrake, or the mandrake itself might have moved. The former seems more likely, but Ritter's firm claim that he saw a running mandrake made the latter seem possible too.

<b>

(If there's a roaming mandrake in the wasteland it's not a problem… but if it appears where people live it could be a disaster.)

<b>

If spotted, it should be subjugated. Before it brings harm to people.
