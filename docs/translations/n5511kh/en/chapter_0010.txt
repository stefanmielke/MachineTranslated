# Chapter 8.5 Noel

<b>

<b>

Noel was a happy boy who lived without want in a beastfolk village. A race with the traits of non-human animals and the abilities that come with them, a people with strong bodies. That was the beastfolk.

But because they have both human and beast characteristics, they aren't thought of very well by normals—by humans. They're sometimes called demihumans.

Because of that, they often make settlements away from human populations. The village Noel was born in was one of them.

<b>

<b>

"Back in the day there used to be lots of magic folk, so it was easier for beastfolk to live, y'know."

<b>

"…Magic folk?"

<b>

"Yeah. If they were women people called them witches, and if they were men they were called magic-users. The magic folk had abundant mana and their bodies didn't age. They lived far longer than we do. They also accumulated knowledge and didn't discriminate between races. There was an age when serving the magic folk was an honor for beastfolk."

<b>

<b>

Noel often heard stories about the "magic folk" from his parents. About five hundred years ago, in the war with the dragons, the magic folk were wiped out leaving only one survivor.

Until then there'd been plenty of them, and beastfolk who served such beings were cherished and happy. After the magic folk disappeared and people who discriminated against beastfolk increased, the world became a much harder place for them to live in.

The survivor of the magic folk now supposedly serves the king, so beastfolk probably won't have a chance to meet them, or so the story went.

<b>

(…Magic folk… witches, magic-users. I'd like to meet one…)

<b>

After that, Noel listened to the adults in the village talk about the magic folk as often as he could.

For example, they tended to master a single type of magic and were called by the name of that magic. They only revealed their true names to people they truly trusted; most magic folk never revealed their real names in their whole lives, according to the tales.

<b>

The magic folk he heard about sounded so alluring, and the fact there was only one left made Noel sad, but it also gave him hope that maybe he could meet that one person someday.

That peaceful world of Noel's ended abruptly when a deadly disease began to spread.

<b>

People in the settlement died one after another. Adults and children alike—one moment they were fine, the next they were dead. Within a week, fewer than ten people, including Noel, were left alive, and those who remained fled the village for fear of the sickness.

His parents died too, and Noel escaped the village himself. But he was already infected. His body wouldn't accept food; he vomited whatever he tried, and he couldn't even drink water.

<b>

(It's over for me….)

<b>

When his body had weakened to the end, his survival instinct as a beastfolk seemed to awaken and his body shifted into a beast. His family were wolf beastfolk, so he apparently transformed into a wolf form—but in his utterly weakened state he couldn't make use of that ability.

He collapsed onto the ground, too weak to move, and all that was left was to wait for death. A light like a lantern reflected in Noel's vision.

<b>

(…who…?)

<b>

Someone lifted Noel's body up. Even though he was in his beast form and had rolled in the dirt, whoever it was held him without hesitation, in gentle arms.

By the dim light there was a very beautiful woman. Her glossy black eyes, like glass marbles, looked down at Noel quietly.

<b>

(…so pretty… like a doll…)

<b>

Like those exquisite dolls a traveling merchant once had for sale. She took a stick from somewhere and put it in Noel's mouth, then placed her own lips to the other end.

A very sweet liquid flowed into his mouth.

<b>

(It's kind of sweet… juice, maybe… since I can't drink myself… she's putting it in my mouth and making me drink it…? But…)

<b>

He thought he would vomit it up again, but strangely he was able to swallow. And as soon as he drank it his body felt lighter. Exhaustion, nausea, the chills—all drifted away, and he became overwhelmingly sleepy.

<b>

(She saved me…? …Could this person be… the witch…?)

<b>

Could she be the one magic folk rumored to be the only survivor in this world? Even a filthy beastfolk child like him had been gently lifted and saved. Only a magic folk who didn't discriminate would do something like that.

Cradled in the witch's arms, rocked as if in a cradle, Noel fell asleep. When he woke there was the smiling face of the beautiful person who had saved him. She looked down at him with a bright smile as if she were truly pleased.

<b>

<b>

"…Th-thank you… very much…"

<b>

<b>

He tried to thank her, but she just smiled silently and kept watching him. He wondered why she was looking at him so intently, and then realized.

<b>

(Oh—my clothes…! I took them off…!)

<b>

After transforming into a beast his clothes no longer fit and had come off; he hadn't had the presence of mind to deal with it and had left them behind when he ran. He was completely naked. Realizing he was being looked at, shame made him curl inward.

The woman then moved her hands as if knitting. With no needles or yarn, clothing began to form in her hands as if being woven.

<b>

(Is this… magic!?)

<b>

A set of clothes sized to Noel was made in an instant. She smiled and handed the garments to him. They smelled faintly of grass, so they were likely made from plants. She had used plant manipulation magic to make them.

<b>

<b>

"…That was… plant-manipulating magic, wasn't it…? You really are… a witch…?"

<b>

<b>

He had dreamed many times of magic folk who could use their powers freely. He'd heard that a skilled magic folk didn't even need to chant spells. His excitement made his words stumble, but she nodded with that same smile.

<b>

(It's the witch…! She really exists!)

<b>

The clothes he received from the witch were simple, but they felt like the most beautiful treasure. But he was filthy and in no condition to wear them—he'd just dirty them up again. He didn't want to ruin the clothes she had kindly made.

The witch beckoned him and led him to the riverbank. When he looked she had a sponge-like plant in her hand—probably to wash his body with.

<b>

(…I hope the witch doesn't go away…?)

<b>

While washing himself in the river he worriedly looked for the witch's figure. She hadn't gone anywhere; she was at the riverbank making a fire.

Her method of starting the fire was unusual. She commanded unfamiliar plants and they began burning on their own.

<b>

(This witch must have mastered plant magic… I wonder what her name is?)

<b>

When Noel finished cleaning himself and climbed out of the river, she waited by the fire and gently wiped him dry with a cloth. She truly held no prejudice against beastfolk. She was a kind person.

<b>

<b>

"Witch, I'm… Noel. Your name is…?"

<b>

<b>

He wanted to be called by name and wanted to call hers. After he asked, she tilted her head slightly and put a finger to her chin in a troubled way, and he remembered: witches don't reveal their true names. They only do so to people they truly trust.

This kind witch had simply helped a dying beastfolk child—there was no relationship that would warrant revealing a name. He hurriedly apologized.

<b>

(This person controls plants, so maybe the Plant Witch? But… those flowers suit her so well…)

<b>

She was beautiful, wrapped in a fragrant bouquet of flowers. "Flower Witch" seemed the right name for her.

Thanks to the kind Flower Witch Noel had been given his life back. But—

<b>

(I have nowhere to go… no family anymore…)

<b>

The village was gone and his family dead. Even if he survived, he couldn't live like he had before. Perhaps if he begged this kind witch she might take him on as a retainer.

<b>

(I can't think of any other way to live… I'll ask.)

<b>

With no home and no family, and wanting to repay her kindness, he wanted to ask to serve the witch. But grief welled up in him and he couldn't say it properly.

He sobbed with hiccups, and the witch hugged him gently. Her hand comforted him as it rested on his head. Who else would be so kind to a stranger beastfolk child?

<b>

(I… I will definitely be useful to you… I'll repay your kindness…)

<b>

Making that vow without a voice, he kept crying for a while wrapped in the warmth of the gentle witch.

When he finally stopped and tried to lift his head, he realized that what had been resting on top of his head was not a hand but her large chest, and his face felt like it was on fire.

<b>

(I thought it was a hand… breasts aren't that soft…)
