Chapter 33.5: Eli

<b>

<b>

<b>

Eli mainly operates within the Elnat territory, and among his clients the largest is likely the Count of the Elnat borderlands.

Because the lord is quite concerned about Bit Village where the witch lives, Eli has been summoned there repeatedly.

<b>

<b>

"The witch is no problem, yes?"

<b>

"Yes, for now. She certainly manipulates plants at will and devotes herself to the villagers. There are moving mandrakes, but when we appraised them they seemed to have been completely neutralized..."

<b>

<b>

The lord had a benefactor living in Bit Village, so he paid special attention to that village near the demon territory. He had been using Eli, who came and went as a traveling merchant, to check whether anything had gone wrong in the benefactor's village.

It was only natural for him to take notice of the suddenly appeared witch. Since the villagers seemed to be drawn to her, Eli kept his distance and observed for a while.

<b>

(She doesn't really look like a villain... but after seeing those stats…)

<b>

When he tried to check the witch's stats with his appraisal skill, the text warped strangely, jittered, and trembled. Eli didn't have particularly strong magic, so sometimes he couldn't appraise things. But in that case the result should've just looked blacked out.

He'd never seen anything like it. Either the person was far above him in rank, or they were some kind of beast.

<b>

<b>

"Keep watching her. The paladin order's investigation says she's genuine… and all the more reason we shouldn't have hostility toward her."

<b>

<b>

Demons are formidable, single-handedly capable of incredible power. Even if there is Nicolaus—the court mage who is a survivor of the demons from five hundred years ago—this witch must have lived far longer than him. If she survived the calamity of the dragon wars, her true strength would be unfathomable. If she became an enemy of humanity, she would be a new calamity. The lord's worries were understandable.

<b>

<b>

"For now she is cooperative. I haven't detected hostility or malice… And, about this—it's jam made from plants the witch created. The appraisal came back clean… I was thinking of marketing it as a specialty of that village."

<b>

<b>

Eli bought a jar of jam made by a young beastman boy named Noel and presented it to the lord as a sample. The status readout showed no toxicity or dependency—it was a safe, nutritious delicacy. Excessive consumption should be avoided, but that applies to any food. From Eli's merchant sense, it was definitely a sellable product.

Desperate to get permission to sell, Eli did his best to pitch the product to the lord. After all, he'd even gone so far as to investigate the witch's identity—he felt he deserved at least this much reward.

<b>

<b>

"I'll permit the sale. My daughter would like it, and I might buy some myself. Oh, and one more thing… have you offer the witch a gift. Don't tell her it's from me."

<b>

"Yes. What kind of thing would you like me to bring?"

<b>

"Something women like… A mirror that shows a true form if someone's appearance has been changed by skills or magic. I received it from the court mage; it should befit a witch more beautiful than I am. He asked me to send his regards too, so make it a gift."

<b>

<b>

Eli felt his smile stiffen. In short, if the witch were some kind of monster in disguise, the moment she looked at the mirror her true form would be revealed.

<b>

<b>

"My lord… if someone who is not a witch appears in the mirror…?"

<b>

"If you've told me there's no hostility, then there's nothing to fear."

<b>

(This is why nobles are—!)

<b>

<b>

He meant for him to take responsibility for his words. There was no way to refuse, and he was entrusted with an outrageous souvenir to deliver on his way back—there was nothing left but to grin and bear it.

<b>

<b>

(I can only pray that the witch is truly a good person…)

<b>

On top of that, he was teleported to the edge of the village using a teleportation magic circle. They must have wanted an answer quickly. Whether that answer was wanted by the lord or by the court mage, he didn't know.

She was an enigmatic woman, but it was true that he had never felt malice from her. The villagers were fond of her. Betting everything on that, he had to use the magic mirror to confirm her true nature and report the result. If Eli didn't return with the report—well, that was probably the thinking.

<b>

(Ugh, merchants have to have guts… If this proves she's human I'll feel a lot better…!)

<b>

He steeled himself and went to the witch's house. Smiling the smile of a salesman, he greeted Noel in the yard, who told him the witch was bathing and to wait a bit. For a moment that small reprieve made him breathe easier, but the witch returned in less than a minute. ...She may have sensed Eli's presence.

<b>

(She always looks so perfectly put together it's almost fake… if she really were fabricated…)

<b>

Trying not to let his fear show, he grinned like a fool and began with light greetings and business talk.

He could read Noel the attendant like an open book, but the witch was unreadable—she always smiled and rarely spoke, so he couldn't even tell what she was thinking from the tone of her voice.

<b>

(My stomach hurts from having to deal with people I can't get a read on…)

<b>

But he couldn't disobey the lord's orders. If he refused, Eli had no future.

<b>

<b>

"By the way, witch, today I'd like to offer a small gift as both an apology and a thank-you."

<b>

<b>

Doing his best to hide his nerves in his expression and voice, he pulled out the mirror the lord had given him. If he showed it in front of the attendant who clearly trusted the witch, he hoped that even if the witch turned out not to be human there would be no reckless mishaps.

<b>

<b>

"Wow… this looks really expensive… the carving is so beautiful. It would suit you very well, Witch!"

<b>

<b>

The mirror reflected the witch's figure. What appeared was the beautiful woman standing before him and the delighted boy. Only then did Eli finally relax. It seemed the woman was indeed human.

<b>

(With this I can get back to business without worry… I was wrong to suspect her, but this is something I had to do to survive as a merchant. I'm sure this witch will understand.)

<b>

It might not have fully been an apology, but he resolved to do what he could to fulfill the witch's requests. The magic mirror would also help protect her from anyone who came close in the future.

<b>

<b>

"By the way, witch. That mirror has a special spell on it, you know."

<b>

<b>

When he honestly explained the trick and hinted that he had been suspicious, the witch smiled as if she understood everything. ...Honestly, she was impossible to read.

<b>
