# Chapter 16.5 Daon

<b>

<b>

The Flower Witch had come to live in the watermill cottage near the village. As she had warned—that if the cause of the illness wasn't found it might recur—by the next morning villagers were again beginning to complain of nausea.

Daon immediately headed to the watermill cottage to seek the witch's help. By the time he could see the cottage, the area had been transformed from the day before: every kind of flower was in bloom, fruits and vegetables had ripened, and the scene was vividly colorful.

The building itself was beginning to be swallowed by plants... In just a single day the place had completely become the witch's home.

<b>

<b>

"Lady Witch, are you there...!"

<b>

<b>

He knocked on the door, but there was no answer. Even when he opened it, she wasn't inside. It seemed she was out. The disease progressed quickly, but it wasn't something that would kill someone within a day. He decided to wait here for the witch to return.

But if people got sick again the day after being treated, the witch's medicine would be needed every day. Would days like this continue from now on?

<b>

(If that's the case... this village will come to rely entirely on the witch. Could that possibly be her intention...?)

If they had to beg the witch for medicine or die, the village would have no choice but to treat her with the utmost respect. They say the witch, who smiles so gently, has lost her voice and can't speak, but perhaps they've simply decided for themselves that she doesn't talk.

<b>

If the cause of the illness wasn't found, this would repeat endlessly. There's even the possibility that the witch herself caused the disease—

<b>

<b>

"Daon!"

<b>

"Huh... oh, it's you. Where is the witch? I need to talk..."

<b>

"The witch is behind the house right now. She found the cause of the illness too!"

<b>

"What?"

<b>

<b>

According to Noel, the beastman boy the witch had with her, some kind of toxin had been flowing in from upstream. The witch noticed it and bloomed flowers that purify the water.

The witch soon appeared herself. She walked slowly, wrapped in the scent of flowers. With her usual gentle smile and a wicker basket in her hands, she looked like a picture—beautiful even with just that.

<b>

<b>

"I really can't thank the witch enough... Ah, Witch. I heard what happened from this child. But already several people have fallen ill; I was going to ask for your help... but maybe there's no need to consult you...?"

<b>

<b>

He had intended to ask her to make medicine to treat the villagers, but the witch's wicker basket contained the same complete-recovery potion as yesterday, and she smiled as she handed it to Daon.

Even up close she was unnaturally beautiful. And her heart must be just as pure and kind. She had prepared medicine in advance, having noticed the recurrence, and had even purified the river—the root cause.

<b>

(I'm pathetic for having doubted her even for a moment. ...I'm sorry, witch.)

<b>

Whether because her appearance was too beautiful to be human or because of his first impression when they met, although he wanted to believe, doubts still lingered deep in his heart.

Why had she continued to live for five hundred years on a mountain where miasma drifted? Although he had come up with plausible reasons, when he went home and thought alone, he wondered whether such a merciful person could really exist in this world.

<b>

(But the more he thought about it, the more he felt that this witch might really be the kind who would do such things.)

<b>

He had repeatedly hinted, trying to see if she had any requests. But all she asked for was permission to live in the village. Even that was an action to save the village... She was almost too devoid of desire.

<b>

He tried to deliver the medicine to the villagers immediately, but a dizziness made him stagger. Daon himself felt a slight nausea; it seemed the illness had indeed relapsed.

When you're unwell, your thoughts easily go in a negative direction. That said, he wasn't going to use it as an excuse for doubting the witch. It was simply Daon's suspicious nature that kept him from clearing his doubts.

<b>

The witch tapped Daon's shoulder, and he turned. Aside from what was in the basket, she was holding a fruit containing a complete-recovery potion.

<b>

(She noticed my illness too, huh. But the villagers must come first...)

<b>

As village chief he prioritized the villagers and, if he could move, he'd be last to take care of himself. Even thinking that, the witch nicked the tip of the fruit. Drops of medicine began to fall to the ground. If he didn't drink it quickly, it would be wasted. So Daon had no choice but to drink it.

The witch had surely seen through Daon's sense of responsibility. But in his head he thought he heard a voiceless voice saying, 'Don't run around in that state,' 'Take care of yourself too.'

<b>

(...Honestly... she's such a kind witch...)

<b>

He vowed never to doubt this person's good heart again. Even if someone else doubted her, Daon would deny it.

He gulped down the medicine, thanked the witch, and ran to the village. He would give out the doses she had handed him and, if they ran out, go back to beg the witch for more.

<b>

<b>

"Ah, Daon. I've been waiting for you. ...I ran off on my own... I didn't have any medicine."

<b>

<b>

Noel drooped his ears and tail in dejection—he was such a good boy. Raised by that witch, his nature would never be twisted. This child would surely grow up to be a kind person.

<b>

<b>

"Alright, then help me from now on. First, the houses with sick people are—"

<b>

<b>

He gave Noel instructions and had him help deliver the medicine. After the supplies ran low, when he went to look for the witch, she was standing by the village well.

The well she touched was quickly covered in green. Seeing the small white flowers, he realized what they were.

<b>

(Purifying flowers, huh. I see—so that's how she purified the river. She must have done the same for the well as it could have been contaminated... Really, she thinks of everything.)

<b>

It's a rather rare plant that normally doesn't grow in inhabited areas. A transplanted specimen decomposes filth and produces fertilizer, but it will never grow further or multiply on its own. That's because it requires a vast amount of magic energy to grow, and aside from demon-kind, there is no race capable of wielding such a large amount of magic to cultivate this flower.

She made them grow in an instant, making them bloom at the well and then at the cesspool. Even a demon would expend considerable magic to make this many flowers bloom and would feel fatigued. Yet the witch simply smiled gently and showed no sign of tiredness.

<b>

(Is she trying not to worry the villagers...? If anyone had mastered plant magic as she had, she might be able to handle it efficiently, but at this volume it should be a burden.)

<b>

Still, the witch bloomed purifying flowers in several places throughout the village. There was a real possibility that the well could be contaminated if sick villagers used it, and since they had been disposing of vomit in the cesspool, the cause of the illness might remain there as well. The witch seemed intent on completely eradicating the disease that had taken hold of the village.

<b>

(Her appearance and heart are both as beautiful as a flower... truly, a Flower Witch.)

<b>

He felt like praying to the gods for the luck that such a witch had appeared in their village.

<b>
