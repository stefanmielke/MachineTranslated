# Chapter 2

<b>

<b>

After crying my fill, I noticed the bird next to me was still convulsing. If it's convulsing, that means it's still alive.

It seemed to have merely fainted from the mandrake's voice. Relieved I hadn't killed it, I reached out to tend to it somewhere safe. Well, technically it was a root.

<b>

(In fantasy depictions, mandrakes either kill those who hear them or just cause fainting or confusion... I guess in this world it's the latter.)

<b>

The moment I touched the bird's body, for some reason it "tasted." It was fairly tasty. While I blinked in confusion at this inexplicable phenomenon, the bird began to wither, eventually becoming like a dead twig and stopped moving. Then that trumpet melody started playing in my head.

<b>

(Could this sound be... the level-up music...?)

<b>

I grew up in an orphanage, but I’d played games enough to know about that. When a character grows and levels up, a melody plays to signal it—and could that be what this was?

Which means... I must have defeated this bird and gained a level.

<b>

<b>

"Meeeeennn...!"

<b>

<b>

Apparently mandrakes aren't built to pronounce words like humans, so when I tried to say "sorry" it came out as a weird sheep-like bleat... I want to be able to speak properly.

<b>

(Ugh... does this body siphon life away just by touching... I'm really a monster...)

<b>

Monster—no, a monster seems more accurate. Plant roots absorb nutrients, and these root-like parts I use as hands and feet apparently draw energy from the creatures they touch.

<b>

(Also, that melody played so many times earlier. Could it be... I massacred a bunch...?)

<b>

This bird hadn't died from a scream, but perhaps resistance to a mandrake's voice varies between species, and some creatures might simply drop dead at the sound.

I realized with a chill that I'd been running through the forest spreading that voice, and could have possibly massacred many defenseless creatures. ...Well, my skin's brown now, but still.

<b>

(What if there were humans among them...!?)

<b>

If they were monsters like me it would be one thing, but if they were humans... As someone who used to be human, the idea of killing people is unbearable—I couldn't do it, I shouldn't.

<b>

(Should I go back to check...?)

<b>

When I looked back it was just a deep forest. From a mandrake's height, it was a dark, ominous wood where trees and tall grasses grew too thick. Just looking at it made me scared, and the thought of going back in to check for corpses made me feel I couldn't possibly go.

Apparently becoming a mandrake has made me very skittish. Whether that's a trait of the species or just my own personality as a monster, I don't know.

<b>

(But what do I do now... I don't really know how mandrakes live.)

<b>

I still had my human memories to the point that I convinced myself I was Hanazono Misaki until I actually saw my form.

My personality has changed in small ways, but my values remain human-like.

<b>

(Humans need food, clothing, and shelter... Wait, at first I didn't need to do anything, right. That was before I was pulled up by that green fellow—I was buried in the soil. So if I burrow into the ground, at least shelter and food should be okay...?)

<b>

And obviously mandrakes don't need clothes. Even if I exposed this plump, chubby round body, it's just roots so there's nothing to be ashamed of. ...Still, this body is very well-nourished. The soil I was originally buried in must have been rich.

<b>

(My flower is really pretty too. Yeah, if you just look at the flower, it's not bad at all.)

<b>

Mandrakes are plants in a fantasy world, so there's no exact equivalent in my original world. But those layers of large, pink petals, like roses or peonies, softly glowing are fantastical and beautiful. Maybe the gods granted my wish. ...Well, I'm not happy if you look below—it's basically a radish with a haniwa-face and limbs. Still, the flower is truly beautiful. I started to remember those tales about beautiful flowers growing over buried corpses and almost began wondering about the nutrients that fed me, so I shook my head to dispel the thought.

<b>

(I'm a plant, so if I bury myself in the ground I should be able to rest. See, I can rest anywhere without a house. I'm exhausted today—I'll rest once and think more later.)

<b>

The soil by the water was soft, so burrowing wasn't that hard. It was just moist enough, and being buried felt surprisingly comfortable.

Maybe I could live here—just as I was dozing off thinking that, I was suddenly yanked up.

<b>

<b>

"Eeeeeeeeek!?!?"

<b>

"Guh...!"

<b>

<b>

I let out a scream at the suddenness. This body reflexively makes loud noises when startled, so it was beyond my control.

I was flung into the air like before and this time landed with a splash in the water. The next moment, I met the gaze of a fish with eight eyes. Of course, I screamed again. The trumpet melody kept ringing in my head.

<b>

(No way no way no way!! Scary!! Can't I swim faster...!? I wish I had webbed feet...!)

<b>

As soon as I wished that, I found I could swim well underwater and I desperately made it to shore. What I saw there was the same green giant as before. Apparently this green giant has a habit of pulling mandrakes out of the ground when he sees them.

<b>

(Okay, I've decided. I will find a safe... a safe home...! Right, maybe there's a human kingdom. In a human kingdom, monsters like this shouldn't be wandering around...!)

<b>

The problem is my appearance as a plant monster... Even with human memories, people wouldn't accept a monster.

Slumping in despair, I noticed my hands and was surprised. Real hands. The tips of what had been thick roots had become hands. And they even had webbing.

<b>

(Why...? ...Because I wished for webbing?)

<b>

Could this body possibly evolve into whatever shape I want? Looking down at my eerie, webbed hands, I couldn't swallow, so I tried to breathe instead... Apparently mandrakes don't have saliva.

<b>

<b>

―――――――――――――

<b>

<b>

In a certain kingdom, in a room of the royal castle. The court mage Nicolaus was staring intently at a potted plant. He hadn't moved for over an hour, so Ritter, a knight sent as his assistant, grew worried and couldn't help asking.

<b>

<b>

"Court mage, what have you been doing this whole time?"

<b>

"'What—? You can tell.'"

<b>

"'No, I don't understand at all.'"

<b>

"'This is why mortals are...'"

<b>

<b>

Ritter felt a little annoyed at the look of utter exasperation. But his interlocutor was a magic prodigy acknowledged by all, employed as the court mage. A knight sent to be his assistant couldn't afford to defy him, so Ritter bit his lip and swallowed the complaint he was about to voice. Everyone in the castle knew the man was an enormous eccentric, so getting angry wouldn't help.

<b>

<b>

"Shall I explain it in terms a mortal can understand... Most monsters just get bigger as they grow. The accepted view is they're too stupid to think of anything but getting bigger and stronger. But monsters with intelligence undergo interesting evolutions."

<b>

"'Huh—intelligence in monsters...?'"

<b>

"'They may not be as intelligent as humans, but some can think to a degree. Like monkeys or crows, there are monsters you could call clever. And such monsters can decide the direction of their evolution.'"

<b>

<b>

Monsters aren't different from animals. No, they're far more violent and less rational than animals. The longer-lived and larger a monster grows, the stronger it tends to become, but occasionally individuals undergo strange changes. Those are called mutants, and Nicolaus apparently studies such mutant specimens.

<b>

<b>

"'But you're talking about... mandrakes?'"

<b>

"'Yes, it's my current subject. I thought plant-type monsters might also possess some cunning. Mandrakes especially make sounds and are closer to animals, right? ...For example, what would scare you if a mandrake evolved into it?'"

<b>

<b>

Mandrakes are plant monsters. But they are also ingredients for full-healing potions, and since they can't move on their own they can be kept and even cultivated. They're regarded more like medicinal herbs that require careful handling than like monsters.

When pulled up, mandrakes emit a scream laced with curses, and those with weak resistance may die just from hearing it. But if you use specially made earplugs, you can easily block the sound. There's nothing to fear as long as you plug your ears before pulling one up. And since it's a plant, even after being pulled up it can't run away, so the damage won't spread.

<b>

<b>

"'I can't imagine being afraid of a mandrake that can't move... Ah, but it'd be scary if it ran around. If you're not prepared to block that voice, you could easily die.'"

<b>

"'Hahaha. For a mortal, that's a good idea: a running mandrake. I'd like to see that... Why don't you try evolving that way?'"

<b>

<b>

Nicolaus talking to a potted mandrake was, unsurprisingly, an eccentric beyond ordinary understanding. There can't possibly be running mandrakes; if such things existed, they'd wreck the ecosystem.

<b>

(They say genius and eccentric are two sides of the same coin... ...But surely he wouldn't actually create running mandrakes, would he?)

<b>
