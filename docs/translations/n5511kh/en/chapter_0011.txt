# Chapter 9

<b>

<b>

After Noel stopped crying, he gently let go of me and fidgeted, looking embarrassed. It's common for boys to feel ashamed of being seen crying.

<b>

(If he eats something he'll probably feel better... Human food, huh. Should I try catching a fish?)

<b>

As I was making a fishing rod out of the plants I produced, Noel kept tugging lightly at the hem of my clothes. Since those clothes were made from my vines and hadn't been separated, I could feel him touching them.

I could detach them now that my pain is blocked, but because the clothes are still connected as vines, I can make plants grow from the tips of my gloves and make it look like I'm using magic with my hands. I don't need to separate them after all.

<b>

<b>

"Flower Witch, if you're planning to fish in the river, you shouldn't. There've been a lot of poisonous fish lately..."

<b>

(Is that so? Then I'll skip the fish. ...It's a waste to throw away this rod though, so, um...)

<b>

<b>

Just then I saw a bird flying overhead. I flicked the rod and extended a vine I was using as a line toward the bird, snaring it. When I pulled it back, the bird was wrapped up in the vine, unable to struggle, dangling from the tip of the rod.

<b>

<b>

"Oh... As expected of the Flower Witch. So that was a magical tool for catching birds, huh?"

<b>

(It's not, but he's looking at me with such respect I should probably just smile.)

<b>

<b>

Noel seems to think I'm an unbelievably amazing witch, so if I just play along I might be okay.

I had caught the bird, but I don't know how to butcher animals. After all, I can eat by just touching something. Noel is human despite his dog ears and tail, so I figured he'd know how. I removed the vine from the rod and handed the tied-up bird to him.

<b>

<b>

"You're leaving the job to me...? I'm a wolf beastman, as you can see, so I'm good at butchering animals. I'll do it neatly!"

<b>

<b>

For some reason Noel looked overjoyed and wagged his tail while gripping the vine. I didn't really understand, but I smiled and nodded. ...So he wasn't a dog, he was a wolf beastman, apparently.

<b>

(The knight I saw the other day had normal ears and no tail... Maybe there are different kinds of humans in this world?)

<b>

They call me "Flower Witch," so there must be people who specialize in magic called witches. There will be a lot of common sense I'll have to learn while living among humans... Pretending to be human looks difficult, but maybe not being able to speak is actually handy since I won't blunder in conversation.

<b>

<b>

"Ah... Flower Witch, where are you...?"

<b>

(Don't worry. I'll be butchering the bird.)

<b>

<b>

Noel looked at me with a frightened expression, so I smiled and patted his head, then pointed at the bird. ...I'm just going to eat a little by myself. Don't worry about it.

<b>

<b>

"…So I should just butcher the bird, right? ...Understood. I'll wait."

<b>

(Yes, yes, good boy. You really do understand what I want to say, that's a help.)

<b>

<b>

He seemed anxious about being left alone, but when I patted his head again his tail twitched a little. Leaving Noel behind, I walked away from the river toward the thicket of trees. Once I was far enough that he couldn't be seen at all, I checked the surrounding presence.

<b>

<b>

"GUOOO!!!"

<b>

(Oh nooooo!? E-Eek, that scared me!!)

<b>

<b>

I had come to look for something I could eat, but a large mass suddenly leapt out from the bushes and startled me. If my vocal seal hadn't been on I would have screamed so loudly I'd have killed Noel by the riverside along with myself.

It was a bear-like monster with black-and-white markings like a panda if you were to compare it to the old world. But it was baring its fangs and drooling, not cute at all. It roared loudly—though not as loud as my voice—and showed strong hostility. It seemed to have marked me as prey.

<b>

(I guess carnivorous monsters attack me as soon as they see me... Maybe I look human and weak to them.)

<b>

Something about me might look tasty to beasts. Thanks to that, I haven't had much trouble finding food so far.

If there are beasts in the forest near the settlement, then I should be able to manage from now on by going out to hunt like this. ...I’m also curious whether human food would provide me with nutrition, so I’ll need to test that too.

As the monster leapt at me, I extended the vines I treated as my hair and tangled it up. I locked its joints firmly in place, leaving it suspended in midair and unable to move.

<b>

(Just a little taste, okay...)

<b>

I pressed my cheek gently against the bear's belly. It definitely had "flavor," and the bear immediately wilted and stopped moving. ...Yeah, this face part functions as a root too. I must be careful never to touch humans with it.

<b>

Having eaten, I quickly returned to Noel. When I came back, the bird had been completely plucked and gutted; the preliminary processing was done. Whether he'd washed it in the river or what, neither Noel nor the bird was stained with blood.

<b>

<b>

"Um, I heard a really loud monster voice..."

<b>

(It's okay, it's gone now.)

<b>

"...Flower Witch, could it be... that you knew there was a monster and went to defeat it...?"

<b>

<b>

That wasn't true—if anything I was screaming inside—yet Noel looked up at me with bright sky-blue eyes. ...That's convenient, so I'll keep quiet and let him think that.

<b>

<b>

"As expected of the Flower Witch...! Um, the bird's been butchered. ...How shall we eat it?"

<b>

(...Come to think of it, we don't have any dishes. For now, let's roast it.)

<b>

"Ah, understood. I'll roast it!"

<b>

<b>

When I pointed at a fire, Noel understood immediately. He disassembled it skillfully, skewered the parts on branches, and began to roast them. He tore it apart with his bare hands, so his claws were extremely sharp and served as knives. He was good at it, just as he said.

<b>

(He gets what I mean even without me speaking—maybe being taciturn won't be a problem. Noel doesn't seem to mind—)

<b>

"Um, Flower Witch... you're taciturn, aren't you?"

<b>

(...There it is!! Of course it bothers you, doesn't it...!?)

<b>
