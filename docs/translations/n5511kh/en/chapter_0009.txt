# Chapter 8

<b>

<b>

(Why!? It was a dog, right!?)

<b>

<b>

Yesterday, the furball I picked up was definitely a dog. But now, curled up against me and sleeping, no matter how I look at it, it's a naked child.

I let out a startled cry, but the lid I made absorbs the vibration and doesn't let the sound out. I can feel the tissue around my face pulling tight, shaping my mouth into a forced smile.

<b>

<b>

"…ah…"

<b>

<b>

The child blinked awake. Our eyes met dead on, so there was no way I could run. Under tousled, messy brown hair, bright sky-blue eyes stared up at me. On his head were the same animal ears I saw yesterday, and a tail grew from above his butt. …Probably, surely, this boy is the dog I picked up yesterday. Why a dog turned into a human is beyond me.

<b>

<b>

"…um…th-thank you…ve-very much…"

<b>

<b>

I couldn't speak because if I talk I might kill him, so I just stared back, wondering what to do. The dog-boy looked confused at my silence.

<b>

(bI'm not ignoring you… I just can't speak or I'll kill you… what do I do)

<b>

As I stared down wordlessly, the boy hugged his knees and curled up embarrassedly. Right—humans are embarrassed when they're naked. And he must be cold.

Then I'll make him clothes, be kind and helpful. I wove vines with my fingertips into a simple garment, tore it off, and handed it over. He couldn't feel pain anyway, so it wouldn't hurt. The boy's eyes widened in surprise when he saw me do that.

<b>

(Oh, shoot! Humans don't make clothes like this…!)

<b>

Living among humans seems like a distant memory, and I slipped into doing something that should have been simple. Thoughts about whether I could bluff my way out, and what would happen if I couldn't, flashed through my head.

<b>

<b>

"That was… controlling plants, magic, right…? You really are… a witch… are you, ma'am?"

<b>

<b>

"Witch-ma'am." That title told me there are people among humans who can do what I just did. If that's the case, I should take advantage of it.

<b>

(…Oh, right. Yeah. I am a witch, a witch. I'm a witch!)

<b>

I nodded firmly. The boy seemed to accept it. Good, that was a close call.

But the boy, happily looking at the clothes I gave him, didn't try to put them on for some reason. Maybe he felt uneasy about something made with strange magic.

<b>

<b>

"…If I wear them, I'll get them dirty… even though you—Witch-ma'am—made them…"

<b>

<b>

His ears and tail drooped like an abandoned dog. He looked like he might let out a weak "whimper…" at any moment.

He was certainly dirty. From a hygiene standpoint, it might be better to wash him.

<b>

(It's better to get the dirt off. It's not that cold this season, maybe he can just barely take a bath.)

<b>

I beckoned him and led him down to the riverbank. I grew a luffa-like plant on my fingertips, tore it off, and handed it to him. He seemed to understand that he should use it to wash himself. He entered the river holding the loofah.

To warm him up, I prepared a small cooking fire nearby. Since making plants as a "witch" wasn't odd, I used a fire-starting herb instead of gathering kindling, and sprouted oil-rich, flammable wood to burn.

<b>

(Being a witch is convenient! Even if I don't seem very human, being a witch makes it okay. Maybe I'm lucky I found this doglike kid before going to a human settlement.)

<b>

Thanks to this kid, I learned how I might be perceived by humans. I realized I could get away with using some of my own abilities without pretending to be fully human, which I appreciated.

While the child bathed, I wove cloth and, when he came out shivering, sat him by the fire and wiped him down. He stayed very still the whole time.

Once the dampness was gone and he was clean, he finally slipped his arms into the sleeves. His fluffy tail wagged contentedly.

<b>

<b>

"Witch-ma'am, I'm… Noel. Witch-ma'am…"

<b>

<b>

He seemed to be asking my name. I suddenly realized I didn't have one. Even on my status it says [Mandragora (Unique)], so I have no name. …I used to be a variant, but after I hit level 99 I became "unique." I don't really know what the difference is.

Also, hearing the boy's name Noel made my Japanese name stand out as odd. Hanazono Misaki would probably draw unwanted attention.

<b>

(Well, I can't speak anyway… I wonder if the words are the same but the writing would be Japanese and understandable?)

<b>

While I worried with my finger on my chin, Noel waved his hands a little panicked.

<b>

<b>

"Oh, sorry. I forgot witches only tell their names to people they trust. I won't ask anymore."

<b>

(Oh, is that so?)

<b>

"Thank you for helping me. …I… I don't have anywhere to go… or a family… so…"

<b>

<b>

Noel clutched the hem of his clothes and started crying. Whether dog or human, he was still a child.

When a child cries it makes your chest ache. …When I was a child, if I cried I was scolded: "Don't cry."

<b>

(I don't know what happened, but he has nowhere to go… Once he's settled down I'll take him to that settlement. Having a human child with me will make me seem more human, and maybe Noel can explain things nicely… He won't call me a kidnapper, right…?)

<b>

I got a little scared and figured I should be extra gentle so nobody would ever accuse me of kidnapping. I hugged him.

I made sure Noel's head was tucked under my chest so none of his body would touch my face—his ears especially mustn't touch it. This way, his body definitely wouldn't touch my face.

<b>

(I should check later what happens if a living thing touches my face…)

<b>

I held Noel until he stopped crying. The tears soaking through his clothes tasted a little sweet, and I briefly remembered my hunger, and just as briefly thought about absorbing this kid thinking he was a dog. I inwardly let out a short "eek." …That was dangerous—almost became a murdering mandrake.

<b>

<b>

<b>

<b>

Well, I am a murderous mandrake, after all.
