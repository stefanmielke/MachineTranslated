# Chapter 11

<b>

<b>

(I want to be able to make medicine from the vines. If I can do that, I think… I could do a lot more things.)

<b>

<b>

So far I could only produce medicine from the root part, which was inconvenient. But if I can make it from the flexible vines, it should be much more versatile.

I wished to be able to do it, so the vines should already have changed to let me make medicine from them. I tried making some and filling it into a berry that had grown on the vine.

<b>

(...Yeah, success! This looks pretty decent, right?)

<b>

A plant that bears fruit like groundcherries with a faintly translucent interior. It's called a will-o'-wisp grass, and places where this plant grows tend to attract ghostlike monsters… or maybe it's the other way round. Since this plant itself often grows near graves and such, it might just draw spirits in.

The insides of the fruit have a lot of empty space, so I tried storing medicine there. As I thought, a medicine-filled berry was formed. It was the shape of will-o'-wisp grass, but instead of the original purple it glowed yellow.

<b>

<b>

"Goddess… what is that?"

<b>

"I'm not a goddess, I'm a witch… But it looks like will-o'-wisp grass, and it's really beautiful. What are you going to do with that?"

<b>

<b>

A girl who had recovered enough to stand by herself was staring at the medicinal fruit with curiosity. It's her first time seeing anything like it, so even Noel couldn't explain. …How exactly do I explain that I'm going to make the sick drink this?

<b>

(It'd be faster to find a sick person nearby and practice on them… I wonder if anyone's in this house?)

<b>

The house right next to where the girl had been lying was unlocked, so the door opened when pushed. Inside I could feel warmth, like the place had been heated.

The girl dashed into the house before I did. …Maybe it's her home. Following her, I saw a woman collapsed in the back room.

<b>

<b>

"Mom, I couldn't bring water but I found someone amazing…! Witch, can you save Mom like you did me…?"

<b>

(If she's not dead, I think it's possible… I'll try.)

<b>

<b>

The woman was in a similar dehydrated state as the girl. I propped her up, plucked a medicinal berry from a vine, poked a tiny hole in the tip, and held it to the woman's lips. The medicine flowed slowly into her mouth, and then the woman's throat gave a small movement, so she must have swallowed it.

I let the woman lean against a nearby bed and stepped back. I shouldn't be too close—if our faces accidentally touched it would be terrible. Instead, the girl sat down beside her mother and watched.

<b>

<b>

"…Wha… why…"

<b>

"Mom!"

<b>

"Ellie…? Oh Ellie, did you come to heaven too?"

<b>

"No, Mom, this witch saved us!"

<b>

<b>

The woman followed the girl's pointing and looked at me. To a child I'm a kind person who saved them, but an adult might see things differently. I nervously watched her reaction.

After staring at me blankly for a few seconds, she suddenly seemed to notice and bowed deeply.

<b>

<b>

"Thank you, witch… I don't know how to even express my gratitude… Um, as for a reward… well—"

<b>

<b>

She muttered something. But I couldn't spend time slowly explaining—I couldn't speak normally and she was weak. And if the villagers were in a bad state, time was of the essence.

<b>

(I'll explain after I've treated everyone. We have to save as many as we can… It'd be bad if they blamed it on a witch's curse…)

<b>

I turned my back on the mother and daughter and went outside. Only Noel followed.

<b>

<b>

"You'll help other villagers too, won't you, witch?"

<b>

(Oh, as expected Noel understands well.)

<b>

"Because witches are kind, you know. I thought that from the start… Please save everyone."

<b>

<b>

Noel had faint tears in his eyes for some reason, and I patted his head in confusion. Come to think of it, Noel seemed to have lost his family; seeing the mother and daughter might have brought things up for him.

<b>

(Once everyone's treated, I hope there's a home that will take Noel in…)

<b>

He misses his family. If he could get a new family, that loneliness might fade. And if a family accepts Noel, and I build a relationship with them through him, I could naturally blend into the village. It seemed like a perfect plan.

<b>

<b>

"Witch! I'll help too! My mom says I should help!"

<b>

<b>

While I was thinking about how to go around the village, Ellie ran out of the house. She'd changed out of her dirty clothes and already looked energetic.

<b>

<b>

"…Witch, I'm more useful!"

<b>

(Hmm? Maybe they're at that age where they want to help.)

<b>

<b>

Fine—I'll have both of them help. I produced medicinal berries from the vines like gloves and gave each of them one. They'd seen how I used them a moment ago, so they should understand.

<b>

<b>

"I just feed it to Mom like you did, right?"

<b>

"There's no other way. Go on, Witch! Leave it to us!"

<b>

"Ah, hey! Some houses are hard to find, so wait—I can show you!"

<b>

<b>

The two kids ran off, and I couldn't stop them. I had only given out one apiece, so they should be back soon.

Since I couldn't follow, I decided to stay around here and not wander too far. I knocked on the next house, but there was no answer.

<b>

(Hmm… what if they're dead inside… I have to check.)

<b>

I extended a vine as thin as a thread through a gap, unlocked the door from the inside, and entered. The fire was still lit and the occupant lay on the bed. …If lights like this were visible, maybe the village had already been almost entirely immobile since yesterday.

<b>

<b>

"...wha, wha… whe… I'm… ho…me…"

<b>

(Oh, they're conscious. So there are people with at least that much strength left.)

<b>

<b>

When I approached, the man on the bed groaned and glared at me, but his voice had no vigor—he was weak.

<b>

(I can make unconscious people drink it, but making a conscious person eat something out of the blue would make me look like a suspicious stranger… Should I just knock them out?)

<b>

If I mixed a sleep medicine and dabbed an almost invisible amount at the tip of a vine, then touched it near his nose so he couldn't see, maybe he wouldn't be suspicious—. As if reading my thought, the man glared at me sharply.

<b>

(He—his stare feels like murder, I'm terrified…!)

<b>

Even though he was near death, his eyes were so sharp they felt piercing. I gave up on drugging him and stepped outside to think of another way. …Or, to be honest, I ran away because I was scared.

<b>

<b>

"Ah, Witch, you're here!"

<b>

"Witch, sorry. The house was locked so we couldn't get in—we came back to ask what to do…"

<b>

(Perfect timing, both of them! Explain I'm not a suspicious person…!)

<b>

<b>

Outside, Ellie and Noel had been looking for me, so I smiled and took their hands, bringing them into the house. Holding hands with children would make me seem less suspicious, and they could explain who I was.

As I expected, when we returned the man only looked a little surprised and stopped glaring at me.

<b>

<b>

"Uncle Daon! If you drink this you'll get better!"

<b>

"Ellie…?"

<b>

"The witch came to help us!"

<b>

<b>

Daon still eyed me suspiciously, but perhaps desperate, he took the berry that Ellie offered. The effect was immediate, and his eyes widened in surprise.

<b>

<b>

"This is… some kind of miracle…"

<b>

"Uncle, we're going to help the others too!"

<b>

"…Ah, go on then."

<b>

<b>

Daon nodded at me. He seemed to accept it.

With the two kids leading the way, I was able to get conscious people to take the medicine naturally. For locked houses I still had to pick locks, but luckily we didn't find any dead people, so I think we saved everyone.

<b>

<b>

"You're amazing, Witch… everyone got better."

<b>

"Of course—I am a witch, after all."

<b>

<b>

When the work was done, the kids flanked me like my bodyguards. I looked like a kind witch beloved by children. No one would think I was a suspicious kidnapper.

It was surely the day I took the right first step as a benevolent witch.
