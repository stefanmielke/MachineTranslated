# Chapter 1

<b>

<b>

The sky full of stars I looked up at from the bottom of the valley was more beautiful than any view I'd ever seen. It was the exact opposite of my current self, who had slid and was now lying on top of a illegally dumped trash heap.

<b>

(What a horrible way to go... My name really doesn't fit this at all.)

<b>

She gave a wry smile comparing the name Hanazono Misaki with her life. Raised in a facility and never having seen her parents' faces, fired from a company full of harassment, she had clung to someone's words—"climb a mountain and your life will change"—and then this happened when she climbed. Sure, her life had changed, but it seemed more like it was coming to an end.

She was utterly alone with no friends. If Misaki disappeared, there would be no one to look for her. She must have damaged something important when she fell; she couldn't move and it hurt terribly. On top of that, she was freezing and her consciousness was already dim. At this rate she'd probably be found someday as nothing but bones.

<b>

(Please, God... at least in my next life let me be something prettier... like a flower...)

<b>

But then again, a flower's life is far too short, so that wouldn't be great either. With that thought, Misaki's consciousness slipped away.

<b>

<b>

―――――――――

<b>

<b>

Consciousness floated back up suddenly. It felt like waking up on a morning when you didn't dream—sharp and instant. But my body wouldn't move properly. My mind was clear, yet it was as if my whole body had been fixed into a mold and locked in place.

I tried struggling for a while, but it was useless, so I gave up. I couldn't move, but I could breathe, and somehow I felt warm and comfortable. Also, without doing anything, I somehow felt "delicious," so it seemed I was eating adequately.

<b>

(Is this what heaven is like? It's not what I pictured...)

<b>

Being able to live without doing anything—no work, no effort—sounded comfortable. Still, it was unbearably boring. I lost track of how long I stayed like that.

It was so dull. If only I could at least move my limbs freely and run around nimbly—.

<b>

<b>

"Ninaaaaa!??!!!?"

<b>

<b>

It came suddenly. Like having my hair yanked, something pulled me out of that comfortable place.

Startled, I panicked a little and screamed at the top of my lungs. I was surprised at how loud I could be—I screamed like I'd never screamed before. Amid that, I thought I heard an odd phantom trumpet melody like "papparapah!" I had no idea what was happening.

I briefly flew through the air and was then dropped to the ground. The sky above was a bright blue, and I understood that I was outside and probably lying on the ground.

<b>

(That scared me... What on earth?)

<b>

I pushed myself up onto my hands, and before I could even relish that my limbs were free, something green filled my vision and made me gasp.

It was a person. A giant with green skin about three times my size was collapsed there.

<b>

<b>

"Eeeeeek!!??"

<b>

<b>

Terrified at that bizarre sight, I screamed and ran for my life. The trumpet melody I couldn't identify kept repeating from somewhere, and even that terrified me and made me cry out.

I frantically moved my limbs, running through towering grasses as far as I could. Where was this place? The grass was taller than me, a strange land. Had I gone to hell instead of heaven?

<b>

(Somebody, anybody, help! Can't anyone hear me!?)

<b>

I shouted louder and harder, praying it would reach someone. I thought I heard a little electronic "ping" different from the repeating melody, but I didn't have the luxury to think about it. The grassland changed into a forest of giant trees.

I kept running, screaming, and eventually the melody that had been repeating in my head started to thin out and then stopped. The strange noises ceased, and I began to calm down. Staggering, I happened upon a spring and wobbled over to it.

<b>

(I should drink some water and calm down... I wonder if this is drinkable.)

<b>

Peering into the spring, I froze at the reflection. Looking back at me from the surface was a brown object with jet-black eyes like a haniwa. On top of it grew a beautiful flower I'd never seen before—a horribly mismatched sight.

I tore my gaze from the water and looked down at my body. My raised hands were the same brown as my torso. My feet were too. To put it plainly, I looked like an outsized radish with arms and legs. But it wasn't a normal daikon—thicker and shorter, more like a round turnip.

<b>

<b>

"I... Nooooooo!!!!"

<b>

<b>

Realizing I had become something non-human, I screamed again. I, or rather Misaki, had never been able to let out such a loud voice. Even when I was beaten or pushed down stairs, I had bitten my lip and stifled my voice. ...So I was no longer the Misaki I had been.

As I wailed with some incomprehensible terror, something dropped beside me from above with a thud, and I shrieked again. Looking more closely, it was a bird twitching in spasms.

<b>

(D-Dead... no, did it faint and fall...? Because of my voice?)

<b>

A noisy screaming plant. Whoever heard that scream would die or faint. I had one idea what this being could be.

<b>

(Could it be... that I've been reincarnated as a mandrake...!?)

<b>

It wasn't that the plants were huge; I was small. After all, I was a mandrake. There was no way a mandrake was such a huge plant. So the fallen green "giant" might not have been a giant at all.

Apparently I had been reborn into another world as a mandrake. In a world that has mandrakes, the whole ecosystem could be completely different. How was I supposed to live from here?

<b>

(God, I did wish, right before I died, to become a pretty flower or something. But I never once wished to be reincarnated as a mandrake…!)

<b>

And so I despaired and wailed like a proper mandrake.
