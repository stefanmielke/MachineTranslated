# Chapter 4

<b>

<b>

Happy at spotting a human, I decided that in order to take on a human form I’d grow by defeating monsters.

Those green giants — now goblins — were easy enough to beat. When you meet a monster you either scream and take it down, or if you can’t defeat it but it’s knocked out, you touch it and it becomes a meal.

<b>

(If things get bad I can just burrow into the ground and still get some nutrients. Not having to worry about meals is a convenient body, huh. Maybe being a mandrake isn’t so bad…?)

<b>

All in all, I was beginning to accept this situation. I’d been despairing the moment I realized I’d turned into a mandrake, but now I’d found a goal, so I wasn’t feeling so pessimistic.

As I happily moved along, something suddenly leapt at me from the side.

<b>

<b>

“Waaaaaa!!??”

<b>

<b>

Something sharp bit into my body and pain shot through me. I screamed from the pain and, through my wildly shaking vision, realized some kind of beast was clamped onto me.

The beast heard my scream up close and suddenly collapsed onto the ground. The recoil made its fangs come loose, but the spot where they’d been sunk in hurt terribly.

<b>

(It hurts… something like blood’s leaking… ugh… heal already, don’t mandrakes have healing abilities…? Can’t you make some amazing medicine…!?)

<b>

I wanted this wound healed right away. I wanted a healing potion. As I wished that, a different sound from a level-up rang in my head: a short “pikon.” …I’d heard that sound before. It probably meant something other than leveling up, but what could it be?

<b>

(Hey, it doesn’t hurt anymore. …Something’s coming out of the hole… a sparkly liquid… scary…)

<b>

Originally the stuff leaking from the hole had been green fluid, probably mandrake blood. Now it glinted like glittering liquid, an eerie sight, and before long the hole sealed itself.

<b>

(Is this, by any chance, a healing potion? It can make it inside my body?)

<b>

So was that “pikon” the sign that a potion had been made? No — I’d heard the sound before and this was the first time a potion formed, so it must mean something else. There had to be another meaning.

The last time I remembered hearing it was when a goblin had grabbed me and I’d been screaming while running away.

<b>

(Hmm… can’t figure out the connection. In a game you’d have logs or text telling you what’s happening… I want a status display or something.)

<b>

Then another “pikon” sounded and suddenly letters appeared in my vision. “Acquired [Status Display],” it said, and I was so startled by the sudden text that I went weak in the knees and let out a little yell. …Don’t spook me like that. Mandrakes are jumpy.

<b>

(This world having a status display makes it feel even more like a game… Well, if it lets me get info that’s helpful. I want to see my status.)

<b>

When I wished that, a bunch of letters filled my vision. It looked like my status was being displayed. I stared at it and sorted through the information.

<b>

<b>

[Species] Mandrake (Variant)

[Level] 86

[Vitality] B

[Mana] Unmeasurable

[Skills] Dispersal Voice, Potion Synthesis, Status Display

[Evolution Points] 62

[Description]

A top-grade mandrake raised on dragon’s blood, containing abundant mana. As a material it can be used to create any sort of potion, but it has undergone a unique evolution that allows rapid movement, making capture difficult.

If you hear its voice you face death by curse, so countermeasures are essential. It has the ability to disperse its voice and can attack with sound from distances it cannot be visually detected from.

※If it continues to grow like this it is highly likely to become a calamity-class monster and is highly dangerous.

<b>

<b>

The description sounded very game-like, or rather oddly tailored more toward humans than from my own perspective. It felt like a system designed to show humans information.

<b>

<b>

“Wait, they treat me like material…? Huh, I can actually speak properly now?”

<b>

<b>

Somehow I could speak clearly now. It was only a few hours ago I’d accidentally killed a bird with my voice and apologized. Monsters evolve fast, apparently. …But becoming able to talk wasn’t listed as a skill.

<b>

(That short sound seems like a skill-acquisition chime. But if it were just my body changing, it wouldn’t be something I “obtained” as a skill. These webbed hands clearly aren’t what a normal mandrake has, but they’re not listed as a skill either.)

<b>

Before I knew it the wound had completely sealed. But looking down, my body still had a dent where the beast had latched on. Would that ever heal?

<b>

<b>

(…Wait. This isn’t a dent from the bite…)

<b>

<b>

There were bumps and curves like a brown radish—bon, kyu, bon. An absurdly tidy waistline.

This was only good as material for a joke to post on social media. And there were no smartphones or blue-bird social networks here. Well, even in my old world the blue-bird social network’s gone now.

<b>

(I did want a great figure, but not like this… if it doesn’t look at least remotely human-sized and shaped it’s just ridiculous…!)

<b>

I slumped down and put my hands on the ground, and my fingertips touched the beast that had fallen nearby. The instant I touched it I remembered the deliciousness I felt and the ability my hands had to absorb others just by touching them, even if I hadn’t meant to eat them.

That wasn’t listed as a skill either, so it must be a mandrake — or plant-type monster — trait. Roots absorbing nutrients is nature’s way.

<b>

(But I can’t touch other living things with these hands, can I. I need something other than root-hands…)

<b>

After thinking for a while, I realized it wouldn’t be a problem if I used upper parts like leaves, stems, or flowers to touch things. Otherwise the goblin couldn’t have grabbed my head to pull me out.

<b>

(If I could grow vine-like tendrils up top that I could move freely…! And then lots of thin tendrils to act like human hair…!?)

<b>

When I wished that, a mass of green thread-like strands fell from my head. I could move them however I liked. Moving each strand at once was too much to process, but if I grouped them into bundles and treated them like thick ropes, it wasn’t so hard. Maybe call them vine-whips.

I glanced at the status display and saw my Evolution Points drop from 62 to 61. …So these points are used to evolve myself.

<b>

(One point per change, maybe. …How do you accumulate these points, though? But with this many I might be able to change into a human shape!)

<b>

Still, I’d feel uneasy without a mirror while changing. I should move to a pool of water where I could see my reflection before changing my form.

<b>

After moving for a while, I found a clear pond where water lilies bloomed beautifully, and eagerly peered into the surface.

My reflection showed a monster: uneven, messy hair standing on end, a brown body with strange bumps, and two black eyes peering out from the green hair like pits of darkness, and of course I screamed. …It looked like a cursed doll with weird presence found in a dump, and I was truly terrified.

<b>

(If it looks like a doll then that’s progress… progress, right… People at least?)

<b>

I should be happy — I’d advanced from a brown radish to a cursed doll, so I’d taken a step toward a humanoid form. …That’s what I want to believe.

<b>
