# Chapter 5

<b>

<b>

<b>

How many days had passed since then? At last I had changed from a cursed-doll form into an appearance that could be seen as human.

However, I still couldn't remove the mandrake flower growing on my head, so I grew other flowers and plants as well and made it look like I was wearing a hat adorned with blooms. I figured that having only a mandrake flower would raise suspicions, so I wished to be able to grow other species of plants and gained the skill "Diversification." If it's a plant I've "taken in" before, I can grow as much of it as I want by consuming my own magic power.

<b>

(Huh… learning a skill cost 10 evolution points, so I was surprised… but I'm glad I had enough points.)

<b>

Evolution points increase by one each time you level up, and monsters seem to use these points to strengthen themselves. …I'm probably one of the few who spent dozens of points just to look human.

Since then I've fought a few battles, and before I knew it my level had risen to 99.

<b>

【Species】Mandrake (Unique)

【Level】99

【Stamina】A

【Magic Power】Unmeasurable

【Skills】Dispersal Voice · Drug Synthesis · Status Display · Diversification

【Evolution Points】32

【Description】

A top-quality mandrake grown on dragon's blood and containing abundant magic power. As a material it can be used to make any kind of medicine, but it has consumed countless monsters for nourishment and gained tremendous power, making capture impossible.

Hearing its voice brings death by curse, so countermeasures are essential. It lures victims by disguising itself as human and approaches while people are off guard, and with the ability to disperse its voice it can attack from distances beyond visual range.

It can replicate various plants and extract their components to create countless drugs, launching status-effect attacks. Do not face it unless you have perfect measures against both curses and status ailments.

A threat to humanity, a calamitous monster. Unless you are a hero, you should run away now to save your life.

<b>

<b>

(Hmm… wow… that description turned out sounding like a villain. Also, who is this description talking to…? And I think I managed to become a fairly pretty human-looking person, but am I scary?)

<b>

I peered into a pond full of lotus flowers. It was a beautiful pool reminiscent of Monet’s pond, but these lotuses were incredibly poisonous — nothing lived in the water. Maybe because of that, the water was very clear and beautiful, so I could see my reflection well. Though it wasn't a perfect mirror.

<b>

Before, my hair only looked like the tattered hair of a cursed doll, but now it had become glossy, forming gentle waves (vines that looked like hair). The green-to-pink gradient can't be helped — it's the vines' natural color. It gives off a strange atmosphere, but this is within acceptable range. I can just say I dyed my hair.

<b>

I adjusted my height based on remembered humans. There’s a limit to how much height you gain per point, so I spent 10 points and finally became about one and a half heads shorter than the humans I'd seen. I gave up spending more points — that would be wasteful. If I'm still unnaturally short for a human woman, I can hide it with high heels. I don't expect to level up again, so I want to keep as many points as possible.

<b>

(Well… when I first reached human height I turned from a cursed doll into a cursed mannequin and that was scary too…)

<b>

It might be best described as the kind of nightmare you'd expect to attack an abandoned shopping mall in a monster-slaying game. The intermediate stages of evolution were something no human should see.

From there I adjusted skin color, removed webbing, reshaped my hands, feet, and face parts, and finally attained a humanlike appearance. That alone probably cost more than ten points. Any remaining oddities could be disguised with clothes and flowers.

<b>

(Clothes too… yeah. From the water reflection, they don't look bad.)

<b>

When my body finally had distinctly feminine curves and a human form, I worried about what to wear. Then I realized, "If I don't have clothes, I can make them." After all, I can freely create and control plants. Plant-based clothing exists, so I could reproduce that.

<b>

First I walked through the forest and took in many plants to find the best fibers for clothes. If I grew them on my head I could change them at will, so I wove plant fibers into garments. …Well, unlike human hair, vines feel pain — tearing off a single fiber or two is one thing, but pulling apart hundreds or thousands of tiny intertwined vines hurt a lot, so I gave up separating them.

In the end, the clothes I wear remain connected to the hair around my nape. I increased the hair volume so it reaches about my waist, so it's never visible anyway.

<b>

(I should avoid exposing bare skin as much as possible. …If someone touches me I might absorb them.)

<b>

The simple, pale-green dress I made follows my body's lines. It needed ornamentation, so I grew flowers on it. …Yeah, it's covered in flowers, but still within human norms. People will probably just think I'm extremely fond of flowers.

Most importantly are the gloves. They connect to the dress and cover my fingertips to hide the root parts. People might wonder how I put the outfit on, but I'm scared to show skin.

It's proven by monster corpses that touching through vines prevents you from absorbing energy from the other person. I ended up with a rather eccentric fashion sense, but the important thing is whether I can live peacefully among humans. Being considered weird is better than accidentally killing someone.

<b>

(I've also taken care of my scream… with this, I should be able to blend in with humans.)

<b>

Maybe because I'm a mandrake, I'm extremely cowardly and a big scaredy-cat. I scream at the slightest thing. So I made a lid over my vocal organs to keep them closed unless I intend to speak. It stays shut unless I deliberately open it, preventing me from screaming on impulse. …Well, thanks to that I can no longer respond to a monster attack with a voice counterattack. Better than killing someone, though.

Also, when I do scream the lid clamps down tightly and it feels like my face is being pulled, locking it into a smile. So whenever I feel like screaming, I end up smiling brightly against my will. …I hope that won't cause too many problems.

<b>

(Alright, off to the human country!)

<b>

I didn't even know where the human nations were, so I wasn't going anywhere in particular. This place seemed to be on high ground, so I decided to walk down along the river. Humans can't live without water, and settlements often form near rivers. …If the humans in this world aren't drastically different from those of my original world, I should be able to reach at least a village.

<b>

(If I meet a human, first I should… greet them?)

<b>

Oh no, I'd completely forgotten how to interact with people. I hadn't really spoken properly apart from screaming. I might need a little practice.

As I walked along the stream, I noticed a rabbit peering at me from the bushes. It was a type of monster, but I could tell it was there because a uniquely shaped horn peeked out from the leaves. As practice, I tried to speak to the rabbit in as gentle a voice as possible.

<b>

<b>

"Oh? What are you doing in a place like this?"

<b>

<b>

No, that's wrong. I should've said "Hello" or something. Before I could reopen my mouth to try again, I saw the horn flop sideways.

<b>

(Huh… so it's not just screaming — talking is bad too?)

<b>

A mandrake's scream causes either unconsciousness or instant death. I assumed that as long as I didn't scream I'd be fine, but that didn't seem to be the case.

There's a chance the rabbit was feigning death. Those killed by this voice have their chests stained black, so I approached the bushes to check. …If the rabbit was already dead, it would mean I couldn't speak in front of people.

<b>

<b>

"Leave… me… alone…"

<b>

<b>

Beyond the bushes, a blood-soaked human had collapsed. …Huh, didn't this person just hear my voice? Isn't this bad?

<b>

<b>

<b>

No matter how you look at it, that's the final-dungeon boss specimen.
