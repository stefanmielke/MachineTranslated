# Chapter 16

<b>

<b>

The next morning, Noel woke up already full of energy. He said he'd work to make up for the trouble he caused, harvested the ripe fruit, and was about to wash it in the river when he noticed the presence of a purification flower.

<b>

<b>

"Witch, this flower… I've never seen it before."

<b>

(It’s a rare flower. Hmm, how should I explain it… ah, that’s right.)

<b>

<b>

I took Noel by the hand and led him to the outhouse behind the house. Not a toilet from my old world, but the kind of simple latrine where waste is collected in a bucket—far more basic than a temporary outhouse.

I don't know whether flush toilets don't exist in this world at all or just not in this village, but during my walk around yesterday I didn't see any. It seems they haul waste to a dung pit to use as fertilizer.

Noel looked puzzled—no wonder—wondering why I had suddenly dragged him to the latrine.

<b>

<b>

"Witch, I'm fine now. I don't feel nauseous, but… huh?"

<b>

<b>

I dropped one of the purification flowers into the latrine bucket. Instantly, flowers bloomed in the bucket, consuming the contents as they opened. They kept flowering until the petals fell off, scattering across the floor. …On the status it says those fallen petals make a very good fertilizer. If you spread them on the ground, they're much more effective than ordinary compost.

<b>

<b>

"Whoa… the smell of the latrine is gone. Could this flower be something that cleans filth?"

<b>

(Yeah, that's it.)

<b>

"If you made it bloom in the river… the river must be polluted, right? Is that what's causing the illness?"

<b>

<b>

As expected of Noel—he picked up on it quickly. I nodded and ruffled his head; his dark brown tail thumped happily.

He's certainly clever. It's inconvenient that he can't speak, but Noel reads almost everything I want to convey. Sometimes things don't get through, but for someone who can't talk, our communication is surprisingly good. I'm glad he's here… though I still get nervous living together.

<b>

<b>

"Still, it's kind of… not like a latrine. A little hard to use…"

<b>

<b>

A beautiful flower blooming in a waste bucket, even forming a ring around it. Not a crane in a garbage heap, but a flower in a dung bucket. Well, I don't have to use this toilet, so it doesn't bother me.

<b>

(My body doesn't excrete anyway…)

<b>

The parts that look like a human body are roots, and while they can absorb, they lack digestive and excretory functions. By the way, I discovered that human food, if touched with my bare hands, seems to only let me absorb the nutrients. I secretly ate a small apple-like fruit and put it on Noel's plate. He popped that slightly faded-looking apple in his mouth and tilted his head, saying, "This doesn't have much taste…?" Apparently, after I eat something it loses flavor. Of course I took it back from Noel and disposed of it in the field soil.

I can eat the same things humans do, but it's less efficient than directly consuming animals or monsters, so it seems hunting living things is still the better option for me.

<b>

<b>

"Witch, are you there…!"

<b>

"That's the village chief's voice. …If the river water is the cause, maybe more people are getting sick. I'll go explain it right away!"

<b>

<b>

What an overwhelmingly capable child. Noel ran out of the latrine and toward where Daon's voice had come from. I walked after him, weaving a vine basket and putting fruits with full-heal medicine into it.

When we reached the front of the house, Daon—looking pale—was listening to Noel explain.

<b>

<b>

"I see, contamination of the water is the cause… As long as the water remains polluted, this illness will keep coming back."

<b>

"Yes. But the Witch can also make flowers that purify water. This morning she made them bloom along the riverbank, so the river water should be fine now…"

<b>

"I truly cannot thank the Witch enough… Ah, Witch. I heard it from this child. There are already several people who have fallen ill—do you need us to consult further?"

<b>

<b>

Daon noticed me approaching and spoke with a troubled look. When I offered the basket, he seemed to recognize what was inside and bowed deeply, tears welling faintly in his eyes.

<b>

<b>

"I can never repay you, Witch. If there's anything I can do, please tell me."

<b>

(No, no, you don't have to go that far.)

<b>

"The Witch isn't the sort to ask for rewards, Daon-san."

<b>

<b>

I shook my head and answered, and Noel supplemented it a little dramatically. Daon bit his lip lightly and looked somewhat apologetic. Maybe he felt guilty, or perhaps it was the embarrassment of being shown too much kindness.

<b>

(If anything, I'm the one with the guilty motive—trying to be kind so I won't be hated…)

<b>

I do want to help the villagers, but if you ask whether it's pure goodwill, that's debatable. I'm a mandrake trying to become a witch who is kind to humans so I can live among them.

<b>

<b>

"Witch, we're coming too, right?"

<b>

(Yeah. I want to grow purification flowers in the well as a prevention measure, and if we put them in the dung pit sanitation will improve.)

<b>

<b>

Noel nodded eagerly and took off running with a proud, childlike grin. Daon watched his back, squinting as if dazzled.

<b>

<b>

"…That boy must be proud, too."

<b>

(Hm? Why?)

<b>

"In these times, to have a beastman serve a Witch like you—that's a rare fortune indeed. Well, I shall hurry as well."

<b>

<b>

Daon picked up the basket of healing fruits and started toward the village, but he wavered a little. Come to think of it, he has been looking pale for a while. Maybe he himself has been unwell. That might be why his eyes seemed to have tears in them.

I stopped him by tapping his shoulder and made a fresh healing fruit. Perhaps he was unaware that he himself felt ill. There are people so sturdy that they judge themselves fit and keep going despite feeling bad, and Daon was probably that type.

<b>

<b>

"Um, Witch… is something…?"

<b>

(Yes, drink this. It'll be okay—you'll know as soon as you drink it.)

<b>

<b>

I nicked the tip of the fresh fruit so the medicine dripped out, handed it to him, and smiled. He looked bewildered for a moment, then gulped it down. That should restore him. Even he must have realized his condition now, staring at the empty fruit.

<b>

<b>

"…Thank you, Witch. I will repay you one day, I promise."

<b>

<b>

He bowed sincerely and ran off toward the village. Walking after the two of them, I felt a little guilty for not being a real witch.

Even if I'm not a true witch, I want to be a good neighbor to humans. I'll do my best to become that.

<b>

(First, clean up the village, right!)
