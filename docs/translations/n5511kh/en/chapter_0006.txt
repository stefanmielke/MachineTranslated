# Chapter 6

<b>

<b>

(Th-this is bad...!! This person is going to die!)

<b>

I immediately crouched down beside the human. It looked like they'd been attacked by a monster and were already near death, but if my curse finished them off it'd be my responsibility. I really didn't want to kill a human.

I unbuttoned his shirt, which had been dyed bright red, to check, and though it was soaked with blood there were no blackened stains. Maybe he hadn't heard the voice, or maybe for some other reason—I didn't know—but it seemed he wouldn't die because of me. He did, however, have a large wound on his chest and blood was pouring out uncontrollably.

At that moment the man weakly opened his eyes and looked at me. His left eye was blood-red, his right a deep gold. Our eyes met for a moment—an impressive pair of odd eyes.

<b>

<b>

"Ha...what a...trivial...life...it...was..."

<b>

<b>

Right—this isn't a time to be relieved. He was bleeding heavily and breathing shallowly. His consciousness looked muddled. At this rate he'd surely die. He'd even uttered words that sounded like he'd resigned himself to the end of his life.

<b>

(It's just like my previous life... I want to help him if I can.)

<b>

In my previous life I couldn't count on anyone's help and died alone and lonely on a garbage heap. Whenever I remember it I really think it was a miserable death. But this man—who would have been dying alone—had been found by me.

All I could do was generate a potion and give it to him, but that's better than doing nothing. Thanks to my Diversification skill, I can produce any plant I've touched. I was born in a very green place and absorbed all kinds of plants, so I should be able to make a medicine even more potent than the first healing potion I made.

<b>

(Um...the combination to make the best medicine...something that heals wounds and restores stamina...)

<b>

Mixing potions is a matter of intuition, so it's hard to explain. I combined several plants inside my body and produced a medicine that I couldn't improve further. Checking the potion's status it read "Complete Recovery Potion", and the description confirmed it was the highest-quality healing medicine possible. If this doesn't save him, nothing will.

To make it easier to get him to drink it, I sat down and placed his head where it rested on my thigh. In short, a lap-pillow situation. It was just the spot where I'd grown flowers, so it should be cushioned. All that remained was to produce it from an opening, so I tried to open my "mouth" with a little snap.

<b>

(No—wrong...! Spraying liquid out of my mouth would be awful from a human perspective!)

<b>

I bit my lip hard to hold back the potion that was about to spill from my mouth. That was close—if I'd been any slower I'd've been a crazy woman about to spit saliva all over a dying human.

In recent fights I'd been spewing poison from my mouth with force, so it was easy to fall into doing the same now.

Lately the types of monsters that appear have changed, and voice-based attacks haven't been as effective. I've been attacked by zombie-like monsters, so I've had no choice but to counter with corrosive poisons. Out of habit I'd almost let it gush from my mouth as usual, but humans don't do that.

<b>

(Um...where should I put it out from...it's building up in my mouth and that's bad...oh, right.)

<b>

If I did nothing it would come out of the holes shaped like ears or nostrils, so I thought to try producing it from my eyes. Snot or ear fluid would be completely unacceptable to the eye, but tears would make it easier for him to swallow. ...Then again, it was doubtful whether the dying man could even see properly, or judge it, but if my impression after he survived was a bad one it could affect my future life.

I couldn't leave behind a tale like "the woman who makes liquids come out of every hole in her face and if you drink them your wounds heal"—what kind of monster would that make me?

<b>

(Get it out from my eye-holes properly...)

<b>

My eyes aren't those clay-figure-like hollows anymore. Originally they were just pitch-black sockets that would leak tear-like fluid when I cried. Why such hollows functioned as vision is unclear, but it's pointless to think too hard about it—I'm a monster, after all.

My current eyes are shaped to resemble human eyeballs, but in reality they're more like fruits in botanical terms. They grow—bear fruit—in the eye-sockets. If I took them out they'd probably make an excellent ingredient for medicine. I'm not human, so they regenerate and could be harvested repeatedly. ...Well, I don't plan on doing that.

<b>

I peered into the man's face and tried to drip the falling drops onto his lips. ...It was very difficult. I felt it would be more effective to make him drink it, but maybe pouring it directly onto the wound would be quicker.

<b>

<b>

"Mmm..."

<b>

(Oh, it went down... Good, the wound's starting to close.)

<b>

<b>

As expected of a "Complete Recovery Potion"—even though bloodstains remained, the wound was visibly closing. His breathing steadied. Perhaps from regaining strength he fell asleep, so I decided to leave him there.

I could have stayed until he woke, but it seemed I could kill someone just by talking. I didn't want to be asked how I saved him or why I was in a place like this—I'd be in trouble.

<b>

(Huh? Maybe he's the same person I saw before? I think one of the pair had this hair color...?)

<b>

Looking again at the peacefully sleeping person, I felt a vague familiarity. He was probably one of the two knights who had entered the forest. There was no sign of the voice's effect on him, but back in the bushes the rabbit monster was definitely dead.

<b>

(The rabbit's dead, so I really can't speak. Why didn't it affect him... Is it because he's human? Do they have some kind of anti-mandrake measures?)

<b>

I checked the sleeping person once more. He had platinum hair and up close his features were very handsome. I wish I could have made my face that presentable, but I had crafted mine while looking at a water mirror, so who knows how it turned out.

<b>

(Is it okay to just leave him like this... Right, at least I'll make a monster-repellent potion and spread it around. ...No, maybe I should spray it on his clothes... That way it'd still have some effect on his way home.)

<b>

So I produced the monster-repellent potion inside me. Although extracting it from my eyes would take time because the openings are small, I decided against producing it from my nose, mouth, or ears because it would look horrible to a human. I cupped my hands to collect my tears slowly and then spread them evenly over the man's body.

I'm a plant-type monster so it doesn't bother me, but carnivorous monsters hate the smell. They shouldn't come near for a while.

<b>

(So please, get home safely. I may never be involved with you again, but...)

<b>

Thinking that, I left the spot. I continued south along the river, and a few days later I finally discovered a human settlement.

<b>
