# Chapter 13

<b>

<b>

We came to a watermill built beside a small stream. A large gear turned slowly, and next to the mill that ran the grinder there was a separate building made for people to live in. Daon seemed to be suggesting that we could live here.

<b>

<b>

"This will be our house…"

<b>

"This place was used by my parents until not long ago. …I kept it cleaned up, so you should be able to use it as is. Although, villagers might come to borrow the mill."

<b>

<b>

Peeking into the dwelling, it did look clean. Even if no one currently lived there, the furniture remained. Daon said his parents used it, but there was no one in the village who looked like his parents…they must be dead by now. Still, I thought he must have cherished and kept his parents’ home in good condition.

<b>

(Is it really okay…? They probably have memories and all, so if I move in and start changing things, will they be angry…)

<b>

As I gazed at Daon thinking that, Noel noticed my stare.

<b>

<b>

"Isn't it an important home? The Witch seems to be worried whether it's truly all right to use it."

<b>

"If it's about my parents, I've come to terms with it. I was only maintaining the cleaning out of habit. A house lasts longer if people live in it. This house would be better used by someone. Please feel free to use it however the Witch likes."

<b>

<b>

I see—so it would be fine to change things to make it more livable. Noel happily started looking around the house. I peeked through the doorway; the furniture from when people lived here was still in place, and there were even dishes, so we could probably start living here right away.

<b>

<b>

"By the way, Witch. I heard you lost your voice?"

<b>

(Yeah, that's how it is.)

<b>

"The villagers can't read, but I can read and write. If there's anything you need, I can gather it, so for now you can write on the ground or something."

<b>

<b>

It's true I can't speak. Noel hadn't asked me to communicate by writing, so I'd been getting by without it, but apparently many people here simply don't know letters.

<b>

(I guess, with this vibe, it's assumed witches naturally know letters. Not writing would look strange… But I only know Japanese… W-will that be okay…?)

<b>

Not writing at all would be suspicious, so it might be more human-like to write something in a script they couldn't read. It could be a foreign script, and they might just assume it's an exotic language.

Besides, I use Japanese for speech, so maybe the characters would surprisingly convey something. Hoping for that, I used a sprouted branch to write on the ground.

<b>

<b>

[There isn't anything I particularly need right now]

<b>

"…Witch, I can't read these characters."

<b>

<b>

What— they couldn't read Japanese script. So even though it's the same language, the writing was clearly different. It looked like proper script, not something a monster had scribbled, but I wondered how Daon would react. He thought for a moment with a troubled look, then sighed as if resigned.

<b>

<b>

"These are ancient characters, right? I've seen characters with similar shapes. I can't read them myself. …Could it be that Lady Witch spent five hundred years without coming down to the human settlements?"

<b>

(What? Why five hundred years?)

<b>

"It was said that from five hundred years ago, only a single magic user remained among the demon race. But you appeared. That there was an unknown witch still alive means…you must have never shown yourself to anyone all this time."

<b>

<b>

From the way he said it, 'witch' seemed to be a subset of what they call the demon race. He distinguished between a magic user and a witch, so maybe the terms differ by gender.

<b>

(So they think I'm a witch among the demon race… …Wait, they live a really long time? It's not surprising to live five hundred years—so not human at all… But there's supposedly only one other survivor? That person is probably still alive somewhere, right?)

<b>

Apparently most of the demon race had died out five hundred years ago. Noel was surprisingly quick to conclude I was a witch. I'm very grateful for that, since it helped this situation.

Precisely because only one survivor was known, people weren't well informed about the species, so even someone like me could pass as a "witch."

<b>

<b>

"That magic user currently serves the king in the capital…working at the royal castle as a court mage."

<b>

<b>

Daon glanced toward some direction. Maybe that's where the royal capital was. The real survivor of the demon race might be there. The thought made me uneasy and my face clouded.

<b>

<b>

"Do you want to meet your kin? You probably didn't know anyone survived. If you've lived away from people all along, that's understandable."

<b>

(No…it's because they're not my kin that I'm worried…)

<b>

<b>

From the real demon’s point of view, a fake would be obvious. In short, people who don't know much about witches are treating me as one, and if the actual surviving member of demonkind saw me, what would they think?

They might notice something off and point out that I'm not truly a witch. If that led to my identity as a monster being exposed—

<b>

(Ugh…so scary. I must hope we never meet…)

<b>

But this village is close to mountains where monsters live, and seems quite rural. The royal capital is the heart of the country, and if the court mage works there, the chance of meeting the real demon should be extremely low.

<b>

<b>

"The court mage doesn't tend to leave the castle much, so meeting them might be difficult."

<b>

(Oh, really? Yay!)

<b>

"But if fate deems it so, you may meet them. The two of you are of the same kind, after all, so surely one day…"

<b>

(No, absolutely not. Really absolutely not…!!)

<b>

<b>

A scream resonated through me—I hated the idea so much. My face broke into a helpless smile. Daon looked at me and smiled, somehow satisfied. From his perspective a lovely story of "two long-lonely kin reuniting after five hundred years apart" probably sprang to mind, but in reality it was more like "the last surviving member of demonkind meeting someone who only pretends to be kin and will rage in betrayal when they realize they were deceived."

That's absolutely not good. I can only see a terrible ending.

<b>

(Please, please let us never meet…!)

<b>

All I could do was desperately pray that.
