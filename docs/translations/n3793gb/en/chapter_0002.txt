# Episode 2: A New Life

After returning to town, I went straight to the adventurers' guild.

I reported that I had left Leticia's party.

<b>

The guild recommends that adventurers form parties.

The reward is reduced, but forming a party also reduces the danger.

To encourage more adventurers to form parties, there are several benefits, such as the provision of items like potions.

<b>

To prevent that system from being abused, things like leaving a party have to be reported to the guild.

<b>

"Huh!? Mr. Treater, you left the Hero's party!?"

<b>

When I told the receptionist, she was terribly surprised.

I had been in the same party for more than five years, after all.

Her reaction made sense.

<b>

"Rather than left, I was exiled."

"Exiled...?"

"I made all kinds of mistakes... Well, something like that."

"I see... Yes, understood. I have received your report of leaving the party."

<b>

The receptionist must have sensed that I did not want her to ask too deeply.

She handled the procedure smoothly.

<b>

With this, I had completely severed my ties with Leticia.

From today on, we were nothing but strangers.

<b>

Thinking that made me feel incredibly refreshed.

At a time like this, I wanted to go back to my roots and do some adventuring.

<b>

"Also, I'd like to take a request. Is there anything I can do alone? Subjugation, gathering, escort work... I don't mind what kind."

"Let's see, um... I'm sorry. Right now, there aren't any requests open for solo adventurers... If you were participating in a party, you would have quite a lot to choose from."

"I see. Then I guess it can't be helped."

<b>

There was the option of taking a party request, but...

There was no way I could handle something like that.

Leticia had often told me I was useless, and that was true.

I was a dropout mage who could not do anything impressive.

<b>

"Then why don't you team up with me?"

"Huh?"

<b>

When I turned around, there was a red-haired girl.

<b>

She was about eighteen, the same age as me.

Her fiery red hair was long, reaching all the way to her waist.

<b>

Long, slender arms and legs.

And yet, a selfishly voluptuous body with clear curves.

On top of that, her face was so beautiful I could not help but stare.

She was a different type from Leticia, but she was a perfect beauty.

<b>

"I'm Alice Sprite. Feel free to call me Alice. Nice to meet you."

"Uh... nice to meet you. I'm Hal Treater."

<b>

For the moment, we shook hands.

<b>

After that, we moved to the conversation area and I listened to the details.

The guild is also a place where adventurers meet face-to-face, so these kinds of conversation spaces are provided.

<b>

"When you say team up... am I right to understand that as forming a party?"

"Yes, exactly. I don't have a party right now either. Because of that, I'm having trouble taking requests... So, if you're all right with it, Hal, won't you team up with me?"

"That would be everything I could ask for, but... I'm weak, you know?"

"Are you? But I overheard a little. You were in a party with the Hero, weren't you?"

"Ah... Well, yes, that's true. But there were various circumstances... How should I put it? I was basically allowed to participate in the party out of pity, as someone who handled odd jobs."

<b>

Yes... I am weak.

Leticia had often called me small fry and useless, but those were not entirely off-base remarks.

<b>

In that respect, Leticia is strong.

She can even subjugate a dragon all by herself.

<b>

"So if you're counting on me as a fighting force... It is pathetic to say this about myself, but that would be a bit of a problem."

"Hmm."

"Uh, Alice...?"

<b>

With a suspicious expression, Alice leaned her face in close.

Then she peered straight into my face.

<b>

"You don't look weak to me, though... If anything, I feel an atmosphere, or maybe an aura, that says you're stronger than I am."

"I don't think so... What level are you, Alice?"

"Twenty-two."

"I'm seven. See? I'm weak, right?"

<b>

I showed her the level written on my adventurer card.

<b>

A level indicates a person's strength.

The larger the number, the greater the power they possess, and the more experience they have accumulated.

<b>

"Huh?"

"What's wrong?"

"Isn't that adventurer card old?"

<b>

An adventurer card is a magical card issued by the guild.

It functions like an identification card and displays the owner's level and occupation.

<b>

I had not updated my adventurer card even once since I first started traveling with Leticia.

<b>

"I haven't updated it at all..."

"For how long?"

"More than five years, I guess."

"That's long!? Why haven't you updated it for that long? It does cost a little money to update, but updating your adventurer card is pretty much essential. It's the best way to grasp your own strength."

"Is that so...? In my case, though, there were some circumstances, and I couldn't update it."

<b>

The reason was that Leticia would not allow it.

<b>

"Haaah? Updating your adventurer card? You don't need something like that. Even if a level-seven small fry grows, it won't amount to anything. Small fry stay small fry forever. So there is no need to update Hal's adventurer card. It would be the greatest waste of money possible, ahahahahaha!"

<b>

After saying things like that, she would not give me the money to update it.

Leticia had complete control of the purse, so there was nothing I could do.

<b>

"I'll update it sometime when I have time. Right now, I'll prioritize talking with you, Alice. It's not urgent."

"Is that so? Then I'll continue, but... why don't we try forming a party?"

"You're awfully fixated on me, aren't you?"

"Hmm... How should I put it? Intuition? I feel like if it's with you, Hal, things will go well. It has nothing to do with strong or weak. I guess you'd call it compatibility. That kind of thing is pretty important, right?"

"Yes. It is extremely important."

<b>

The matter with Leticia had made me realize that painfully well.

<b>

"For now, why don't we try taking one request? Something simple that two people can do. If it seems like it will work, then great. If it seems hopeless, then we stop there. How about it?"

"Let's see... All right, let's give it a try."

<b>

And so, I ended up forming a trial party with Alice.

<b>

<b>

<b>

----------------

<b>

<b>

<b>

The request we accepted was to subjugate monsters that appeared outside town.

Our target was a dog-type monster called a hound wolf.

<b>

The recommended subjugation level was five, so it was not a serious enemy.

It would be a good exercise to check my compatibility with Alice.

<b>

"Hah!"

<b>

Alice cut down a hound wolf with her sharp sword.

The sword strike, hiding power beyond what her appearance suggested, bisected the monster's body.

<b>

However, there was not only one enemy.

Two remained.

<b>

One of them approached Alice just after she had launched her attack, while she could not move.

It bared its fangs and leaped at her with a growl...

<b>

"Alice!"

<b>

I used a sword one size smaller than Alice's as a shield and stopped the hound wolf's charge.

<b>

"Nice one, Hal!"

<b>

I had managed to buy time for Alice to recover her stance.

<b>

Alice turned a refreshing smile toward me...

Then glared at the enemy.

<b>

"Sword Dance!"

<b>

Alice's sword technique activated.

With movements like a dance, she sliced the hound wolf's body to pieces.

<b>

"Grrraah!"

<b>

The final hound wolf, driven into a corner, charged in desperation.

<b>

"Take this!"

<b>

I threw a throwing knife and stopped the hound wolf's movement.

<b>

"Double Slash!"

<b>

Alice's sword flashed twice, finishing off the last one.

She stayed alert to our surroundings...

And after confirming there were no more enemies, she sheathed her sword.

<b>

Then she showed me a very bright smile.

<b>

"That was amazing!"

"Huh?"

<b>

What was she talking about?

<b>

"I didn't do anything impressive, did I? I only focused completely on support."

"That's not true. I think it's impressive enough that you're a mage who can use a sword. More than anything, you attack at exactly the timing I want, and you cover me when I think things are getting dangerous... How should I put it? A master of support? I don't think there are many people who can move like you, Hal."

"Well... I'm used to it."

<b>

When I was in Leticia's party... I had always devoted myself to support.

Leticia never allowed me to step forward.

<b>

At first, I thought she was worried about me, but I was wrong.

Leticia had not wanted me to receive any credit, no matter how small.

No matter how small the achievement, she had wanted it all for herself.

<b>

I was disgusted by Leticia's bottomless greed.

When had she become so twisted...?

<b>

"Hal? Is something wrong?"

"No... it's nothing. More importantly, are you hurt?"

"Ah, this? Just a little scrape from earlier... It's not a serious injury."

"If it festers, that would be bad. I'll treat it right away."

"But using a potion for something like this would be..."

"It's fine. I'll use magic."

"Huh?"

<b>

While Alice stared blankly for some reason, I chanted recovery magic.

<b>

"Heal."

<b>

A pale light enveloped Alice's wound, healing it as if time itself were rewinding.

Before long, the wound vanished completely.

<b>

"There, all done."

"..."

"What's wrong, Alice?"

"What's wrong? Everything is wrong... Wh-what is going on!?"

"Wh-what? Why are you so surprised?"

"Of course I'm surprised! Why can you, a mage, use recovery magic? Recovery magic should only be usable by priests and a few other limited occupations! Mages can only use attack magic. That's how it's supposed to be!"

"Is that so?"

"It is! Why don't you know something that basic?"

"Even if you ask me why..."

<b>

Without a doubt, it was Leticia's fault.

<b>

Leticia had denied me the chance to acquire the general knowledge adventurers were supposed to have.

Someone like you is a dropout, so doing that has no meaning and is a waste of time... she would say.

She took away my chance to learn, and unable to argue, I simply stayed that way.

<b>

It was not as though I knew nothing at all, but...

Because of Leticia, I had not known that mages were supposedly unable to use recovery magic.

<b>

"Hal, there are parts of you I really don't understand. You were in the Hero's party, right? What kind of life were you living there?"

"That is..."

<b>

Even if I told her the truth, would she believe me?

I was hesitating over my answer when it happened.

<b>

"Grrrrrr...!!!"

<b>

With its voice trembling in anger, an even larger hound wolf appeared.
