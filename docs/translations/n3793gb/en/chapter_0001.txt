# Episode 1: I Decided to Get Myself Exiled

I wrote this intending it to be a slightly long short story.

----------------

<b>

"Hal, are you kidding me!? You got scared facing a low-ranked small-fry monster like that and failed your magic chant... What were you thinking!? Hal, are you really such trash that you're worse than that kind of small fry? Ah, ah, then I suppose I'm the one at fault. If it was a small fry like that, I thought even useless Hal would somehow manage, but I never imagined you'd be this worthless! You're so useless I'm honestly shocked. Seriously, I can't believe it."

<b>

My childhood friend was raging right in front of me.

She was hurling every insult she had at me, Hal Treater.

<b>

Her name was Leticia Platinas.

She was a first-class adventurer who bore the title of hero.

<b>

To the public, she was said to be a goddess descended to earth.

She not only possessed great power, but also a deeply compassionate heart.

<b>

She also had looks worthy of the name goddess.

If ten men were present, every one of them would turn back and stare.

One adventurer described Leticia as a beauty completed like a sculpture.

<b>

And the true face of the hero praised in that way was...

<b>

"Ah, so that's how it is. I get it now. Hal, you're trash far beyond anything I expected. I wanted that kind of joke to stop at your face, but it seems that isn't the case. If that's how it is, then I'm sorry. I'm sorry, Hal. I never thought you were trash among trash... the king of trash."

"I'm sorry."

"Don't just say you're sorry. Is apologizing the only thing you can do? Because of you, my face almost got scratched! At a time like that, you should become my shield and die in my place!"

<b>

That was how she was.

<b>

People said she was kind like a goddess, but that was a false mask.

Her personality was twisted through and through like this.

Because she kept up such a good public face, no one but me knew Leticia's true nature.

<b>

We had companions, but...

Since they were away from their seats right now, Leticia could say whatever she wanted.

<b>

I once told our companions about the situation, but...

They wouldn't believe Leticia could ever do something like that.

Truly, only her outward face was the perfect hero.

<b>

"Hey, instead of staying silent, why don't you say something? Is your head so empty you've forgotten how to talk?"

"..."

"Fine. Hal, I'll teach you the line you ought to say. 'I'm sorry, Lady Leticia, for making a mistake so difficult that even trash could hardly make it'... There."

"..."

"Come on, hurry up and say it. Otherwise, I don't know what I might do in my anger. Hey, are you listening? Hal!!!"

"I'm listening."

"Eh..."

<b>

Without flinching at Leticia's abuse, I looked straight back into her eyes.

<b>

That was a reaction she had never seen from me before.

Because of that, Leticia flinched slightly.

<b>

But she immediately put an arrogant smile on her lips and spoke in a forceful tone.

<b>

"If you were listening, then hurry up and do it, would you? I hate being kept waiting. Come on, hurry. Oh, and don't forget to prostrate yourself."

"...No."

"Huh? What did you just say?"

"I'm not going to obey Leticia anymore."

<b>

Leticia looked blank, as if she had just seen a bird speak.

<b>

Then she grabbed me by the chest and glared at me.

<b>

"Are you mocking me? I am telling you to apologize. Useless, worthless Hal should immediately obey."

"That's why I can't do that anymore."

<b>

Leticia and I were childhood friends, and we had known each other since before I could remember.

At first, she had an ordinary personality.

<b>

But as we walked the path of adventurers together...

From the moment she was chosen as the hero, Leticia began to change.

<b>

She constantly denied my worth and branded me as worthless.

Not only that, she hurled abuse at me every day.

She probably thought of me as some kind of practice dummy.

By doing that, she must have been venting her stress.

<b>

I had continued traveling with that Leticia for more than five years.

Without arguing back, I showed obedience and did as I was told...

I did everything exactly as Leticia said.

<b>

Why had I done that?

<b>

I think, somewhere inside, I believed in Leticia.

No... I think I wanted to believe in her.

<b>

That someday, the kind Leticia from long ago would come back...

That we would be able to laugh from the heart together again, just like before...

<b>

But that reality was impossible.

Leticia only grew more arrogant...

And repeated her harassment every day.

She never returned to the way she had been. If anything, she kept drifting farther away.

<b>

And then came today's line.

Die for me.

<b>

The old Leticia never would have said that.

She hadn't been the kind of girl who would say such a thing even if her mouth were torn open.

<b>

But... that Leticia no longer existed.

She was probably dead.

<b>

The moment I recognized that...

The part of me that still obeyed this Leticia suddenly felt unbearably foolish.

<b>

Let's stop already.

Let's bring this kind of time to an end.

It would be better not only for me, but probably for Leticia too.

<b>

So I would end it.

Today, I would part ways with Leticia.

<b>

"Heh... For someone like Hal to defy me, you've got some nerve. Your punishment is decided. I'll train you so you never think of doing anything that ridiculous again."

"That's why I'm not listening to Leticia anymore."

"Are you sure you want to say that? I'm generous enough to let someone as useless as Hal stay in my party, you know? If you're going to talk back like that... maybe I should exile a dropout like you."

"Understood. Then goodbye."

"Eh?"

<b>

I had been waiting for those words.

I had deliberately made a mistake against a weak monster to bring things to this point.

I had been testing her on purpose.

<b>

And the result was...

Die in my place.

She was hopeless to the bitter end.

<b>

"As Leticia said, I'll leave the party. Thank you for everything until now."

"Eh? No, wait... eh?"

"I'll file the application to cancel my party registration. So Leticia doesn't need to do anything. Oh, right. Handle the report for this request yourself, all right? I have nothing to do with Leticia anymore."

"No, that's not... Wait!"

<b>

Leticia shouted with panic in her voice and a flustered expression.

It seemed she still hadn't swallowed reality.

<b>

"Hal, what ridiculous thing are you saying? Leave my party? I don't remember giving you permission for that."

"I don't need Leticia's permission, do I? When we formed the party, we had to report it to the guild so there wouldn't be problems later... But I've never heard that permission is needed to leave."

"That isn't what I'm talking about! Even though you're Hal, you intend to defy what I say!? Are you serious!?"

"I'm serious."

"...!?"

<b>

When I glared at her hard, Leticia flinched for an instant.

<b>

"I can't stay with Leticia anymore. I've thought that for a long time... but today made up my mind completely. There is no chance I'll take it back. Leticia and I... are strangers from today on."

"Wh-what? With that serious face... Why are you getting that angry? Are you mad because I called you trash? Or could you not stand being called useless? Fine, then. I'll apologize."

"...You really don't understand anything."

"Wh-what..."

<b>

Toward this Leticia...

I felt no anger.

I was only sad.

Being told to die by the childhood friend I had once been so close to... was unbearably sad and lonely.

<b>

"This is goodbye."

<b>

I shouldered the bag I had prepared in advance and turned my back to Leticia.

<b>

"Hal, are you serious!? There's no way trash like you can get by after leaving my party! The world isn't that kind!"

"What I do is for me to decide. It isn't for Leticia to decide."

"Guh... Wait! Take back what you said right now! If you do it now, I'll forgive you. If you cry and beg, I might even let you back into the party!"

"Even at this point, you're still saying things like that..."

"I'm the hero! Leaving my party is the same as making an enemy of me! Do you think you'll get away with doing that for free!? I mean, I won't let you get away with it for free!"

"Goodbye."

<b>

Without looking at Leticia as she screamed and ranted, I began walking forward.

<b>

"Wait, Hal!? Don't screw with me. I will never accept this! Hal!!!"

<b>

Leticia did not change. She shouted just as she always had.

Listening to my former childhood friend's voice, like a scream, I left that place behind.

<b>
