# Chapter 9: Forming a Party

"Welcome back."

<b>

When I returned to the inn, Alice greeted me with a smile.

Without thinking, I just stared blankly.

<b>

Seeing me like that, Alice tilted her head slightly in puzzlement.

<b>

"What's wrong?"

"Ah, no..."

<b>

How long had it been since someone had said "welcome back" to me?

For some reason, I felt incredibly nostalgic.

<b>

"...I'm home."

"Yeah."

<b>

In that instant, the strength went out of my legs.

Maybe the damage from my fight with Leticia had finally caught up with me.

<b>

I collapsed as if crumbling to the floor...

<b>

"Whoa there."

<b>

Before I could, Alice caught me.

<b>

"Are you okay? Now that I look closely, you're all beat up."

"Well... a lot happened."

"Mm, that's fine. I mean, I am curious, but I'll leave it up to you whether you want to talk about it."

"...Are you sure that's okay?"

"It is."

"Thank you."

<b>

That was the limit of what my consciousness could take...

<b>

"Good work, Haru. And good night."

<b>

As if being embraced by Alice's gentle voice, I fell asleep.

<b>

<b>

<b>

----------

<b>

<b>

<b>

The next morning.

<b>

"I'm sorry!"

<b>

After we moved to the dining hall on the first floor, I lowered my head to Alice and apologized.

<b>

We had been in the middle of discussing whether or not to form a party, yet I went off on my own and did whatever I pleased...

Then I came back a wreck, fell asleep without permission...

And to top it off, I ended up using the same room as Alice, whom I had only just met.

<b>

I had been thoughtless in all sorts of ways.

Worse, I had even made her worry about me... I was hopeless.

<b>

"Hm? Why are you apologizing?"

<b>

But Alice looked blankly confused, as if she had no idea why I was apologizing.

When I explained what I had done wrong,

<b>

"It doesn't bother me at all, you know?"

<b>

She said that quite readily.

<b>

"If I really hated it, I'd say so properly when that happened. So when I don't say anything, it means I don't think anything of it. Don't worry about it."

"Even if you say that..."

"Let's talk about something more constructive instead."

<b>

She treated it as a trivial matter and made it as if it had never happened.

Was she broad-minded, or should I call this rough and easygoing...? Which was it?

<b>

"Something constructive?"

"The party."

"Ah."

"That face says you completely forgot, doesn't it?"

"...I'm ashamed."

<b>

The fact that I had been abused.

The fact that I had parted ways.

And the fact that I had confronted Leticia.

<b>

So many things happened yesterday that I had completely forgotten.

<b>

"For now, yesterday was only a trial run... What do you think, Haru? For the record, I want to form a party with you."

"I... Yeah. I want to form a party with you too, Alice."

<b>

It was not just that our timing fit perfectly.

Being with Alice was fun, and it let me laugh naturally...

So, if possible, I wanted to stay with her from now on too.

<b>

Maybe choosing a party for that kind of reason was wrong.

But personally, I thought it was an important point.

<b>

When I told her that, Alice smiled happily.

<b>

"I see. Thank you. I'm happy to hear you say that."

"Then..."

"But are you really sure about me?"

"Hm?"

"I'm level 22, and my lower-class job is swordswoman. From your perspective, Haru, I think I'm nothing but dead weight."

"I want Alice."

<b>

When I answered immediately, Alice's eyes widened in surprise.

<b>

"This might sound exaggerated, but I feel like you saved me, Alice. So I want to keep being with you."

"...Okay, understood. Then I'll be counting on you from now on."

"Same here."

<b>

We shook hands and smiled at each other.

<b>

<b>

<b>

----------

<b>

<b>

<b>

Strike while the iron is hot.

Having decided to form a party, we immediately headed to the Adventurers' Guild and reported it.

There were no particular problems, and they accepted it without issue, officially forming our party.

<b>

"Since we're here, should we look for a request while we're at it?"

"No."

<b>

When I suggested that offhandedly, she rejected it instantly.

<b>

"Now that we've officially formed a party, there are all sorts of things we need to do."

"...Like what?"

"Huh? You don't know?"

"I don't... Sorry. I lack a lot of the knowledge adventurers are supposed to have as a matter of course."

<b>

By spending time with Alice, I realized that I lacked knowledge.

So I had no idea what Alice meant by the things we had to do.

<b>

"Hmm... Sorry! Yesterday I said you didn't have to force yourself to talk about it, but I guess I am curious about what you've been through, Haru. It also affects what we do from here on, so if you can, I'd like you to tell me."

"You're right... Yeah. I was thinking I wanted you to know too, Alice, so this works out."

"Thank you. Let's go back to the inn and talk there. I'll explain things about what we need to do from now on too."

<b>

With our future plans decided, we were about to leave the Adventurers' Guild...

<b>

"Found you, Haru!"

<b>

The doors of the Adventurers' Guild opened, and Leticia appeared.
